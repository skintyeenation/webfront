# Education

This chapter of the ISC BC Region 2024-2025 Program Guide covers funding for elementary, secondary, and post-secondary education of First Nations learners. Most elementary/secondary funding flows through the BC Tripartite Education Agreement (BCTEA) formula and is driven by the annual **Nominal Roll** count; post-secondary funding flows through PSSSP/UCEPP and the Post-Secondary Partnerships Program. The primary contact for nearly all programs is the **ISC BC Region Education Programs unit** (bceducation@sac-isc.gc.ca, 604-775-5100, toll-free 1-800-567-9604), with FNESC/FNSA administering several programs on ISC's behalf.

> **Note on terms:** Within First Nations' Chart of Accounts, "public school(s)"/"public tuition" may appear as "provincial school(s)"/"provincial tuition," and "First Nation school(s)" as "band-operated school(s)."

## General Information & What's New

- **BCTEA expiry:** The current BCTEA (Supporting First Nation Student Success) expires June 2025; a successor agreement is being negotiated. See www.fnesc.ca/bctea.
- **"School age student" redefined (ages 4-21):** Effective 2023-2024, the Nominal Roll eligible age range was raised from 4-19 to **4-21** to align with BCTEA. Students aged 20-21 now generate funding under several BCTEA components (English language learning, Indigenous education, student location factor, language and culture, rural/climate/small community supplements).
- **Targeted Adult Education Enhancement Funding (2023-24 to 2026-27):** Limited-time funding (Budget 2021) to expand secondary completion/upgrading for adult learners (aged 22+) on reserve, distributed as amendments to funding arrangements.
- **Before- and After-School Programming (BASP):** \$515M national investment over five years; in BC, FNESC administers this via its Impacts on Student Learning Grants.
- **Menstrual Products funding** (see below) and the **IAFNYES pilot** (see below) are also highlighted.
- Connect with ISC at bceducation@sac-isc.gc.ca *before* adding to the Nominal Roll students in new grades/adult programs, new facilities, or alternative education.

## Programs Administered by FNESC and FNSA

ISC programs delivered by the First Nations Education Steering Committee (FNESC) and First Nations Schools Association (FNSA). Proposal/application/reporting deadlines are on www.fnesc.ca and www.fnsa.ca.

- **First Nations and Inuit Youth Employment Strategy (FNIYES):** Educational and summer work opportunities for First Nations and Inuit youth (incl. post-secondary). Two streams — Skills Link and Summer Work Experience (incl. Co-operative Education). Apply via FNESC calls for proposals.
- **Special Education Program (SEP) for First Nations Schools:** Per BCTEA, SEP funding for First Nation schools is administered by FNESC (work plan, application, reporting at www.fnesc.ca/programs/special-education/). For public and private/independent schools, special education is included in the First Nation Student Rate.
- **BC First Nations Post-Secondary Partnerships Program (PSPP):** First Nations-directed, proposal-driven; administered by FNESC with the Indigenous Adult and Higher Learning Association (IAHLA). (Detailed under Post-Secondary below.)

## Services Administered by FNESC and FNSA

- **First Nations School Assessments:** A school evaluation must be completed every 5 years (FNSA Assessment & Certification Process, a BC Independent School Act inspection, or a mutually agreed alternative). Contact FNESC 1-877-422-3672 / fnsa@fnesc.ca.
- **First Nations Language and Culture Program (FNLCP):** Per-capita (Nominal Roll, K4-Grade 12) language/culture funding for students at First Nation schools, accessed by submitting work plans. Details at www.fnesc.ca/first-nations-languages/.
- **Second/Third Level Services:** Curriculum supports, student assessments, parents club, Connected Classrooms Initiative, data management, plus online resources (curriculum, Education Coordinator support, BCTEA bulletins).

## Elementary/Secondary Nominal Roll

The Nominal Roll is the registry of all eligible elementary/secondary students funded to attend a First Nation, public, or private/independent school — eligibility must be demonstrated before funding.

- **Eligibility (all three required, as of September 29):**
  1. Enrolled in a public school, ISC-approved First Nation school (incl. Adult Education/Learning Centre and Early Childhood K4 programs), or a province-recognized private/independent (Group 1 & 2) school.
  2. Age 4+ (no upper limit for adult learners) on December 31: K4 (age 4); School Age (4-21, K4-Grade 12); Adult (18+, in a BCTEA Adult Graduation Diploma program).
  3. Status First Nations student ordinarily resident on reserve/leased reserve land, OR non-status/other Indigenous, OR non-Indigenous student ordinarily resident on reserve.
- **Funding cycle (2024-2025):** Paid by agreement type — Contribution Agreement (30% Apr, adjustment Jun, 40% Sep, 30% Jan), New Fiscal Relationship Grant and Block (30% Apr, adjustment Jun, 70% Sep).
- **Junior Kindergarten (K4):** First Nation schools may offer full-time K4 within K-12 (child turning 4 by Dec 31).
- **K-Grade 12 Reciprocal Tuition:** Under the 2009 BC/FNESC agreement, the Ministry pays reciprocal tuition for off-reserve students at FNSA-certified/BC Independent (Group 1 or 2) First Nation schools not federally funded on the Nominal Roll.
- **Adding grade(s)/program(s):** Apply to ISC; accepted year-round but submit by **January 31** for the following school year.
- **Education Information System (EIS):** ISC database/portal for funding requests and reporting; requires training and access (BCFundingServices-cbservicesdefinancement@sac-isc.gc.ca).

## First Nation Schools — BCTEA Funding Model

Formula-based funding (based on the provincial formula with First Nations adaptations) for educating eligible on-reserve students (K4-Grade 12) at First Nation schools — instructional costs, salaries, books, supplies, low-cost special education, language/curriculum development, O&M, and transportation (the operating First Nation handles student transportation). Calculated using the annual Nominal Roll count; recipients receive funding for all attending students regardless of reserve of residence. Funding factors/rates (2022-2023 reference table) include a Basic Allocation Rate of \$7,885, Language and Culture \$1,195, plus enrolment-decline, small-community, and transportation supplements. BCTEA Funding Manual at www.fnesc.ca/bctea.

- **Report (DCI):** Nominal Roll Report — due **October 15**.

## Public Tuition

Education services for eligible on-reserve students (Kindergarten Age 5-Grade 12) attending public schools. First Nations with a Local Education Agreement (LEA) may have funds provided directly; without an LEA, ISC pays the province directly. Initial allocations use the prior Nominal Roll and First Nation Student Rates, adjusted in June.

- **Report (DCI):** Nominal Roll Report & Education Staff Census Report — due **October 15**.
- New/renewed/extended LEAs are due to ISC by **October 15**; First Nations must notify ISC in writing within 30 days of an LEA's early termination or upcoming expiry.

## Independent/Private Schools Off Reserve

Tuition funding for eligible on-reserve students (K5-Grade 12) at off-reserve independent/private schools in Group 1 or 2 categories; based on Nominal Roll students at 100% of the corresponding First Nation Student Rate (which includes special education). First Nations are strongly encouraged to have an LEA; those with more than 10 students at an independent school **must** have an LEA (toolkit at www.fnesc.ca/lea/).

- **Report (DCI):** Nominal Roll Report & Education Staff Census Report — due **October 15**.

## Ancillary Services (Public/Private Tuition)

Supplementary services (school supplies, equipment, specialized educational services, specialized-class fees) for eligible on-reserve students at public/non-First Nation independent schools. Rate **\$287 per student/year** (Nominal Roll; adults eligible). *Example:* 44 students × \$287 = \$12,628.

- **Report (DCI):** Nominal Roll Report & Education Staff Census Report — due **October 15**.

## Comprehensive Instructional Elementary/Secondary Support Services (CISS)

Additional student support funding for all recipients regardless of school type (First Nation, public, independent) — toward supplies, accommodation, transportation, financial assistance, or guidance/counselling. Rate **\$221 per student/year** (Nominal Roll; adults eligible). *Example:* 44 × \$221 = \$9,724.

- **Report (DCI):** Nominal Roll Report & Education Staff Census Report — due **October 15**.

## Student Accommodation Services

Funding for school-aged students (4-21, K4-Grade 12) who must leave their home community to attend school. **Adult students (21+) are NOT eligible.**

- **Eligible circumstances:** No school with appropriate grades/program within reasonable travel distance; need for access to medical services (annual application + medical certificate); or "Other" unique circumstances (with written documentation).
- **Funding:** Based on actual approved students × \$8,211 (5-day non-remote), \$10,217 (7-day non-remote), or \$14,562 (avg remote/isolated). Component rates include Room & Board (\$6,480 / \$4,629), Ancillary \$280, Transition Support \$1,436, Travel Chaperone, Criminal Record Check \$28, Bus Pass \$600, Seasonal Travel; 5% administration added. *Example:* 15 × \$10,217 + 5% = \$160,918.
- **Apply (PAW):** For a new request or changed circumstance, email Education Programs with supporting documentation; reflect on the Nominal Roll Student and Education Staff Census Report — due **October 15**.
- **Report (DCI):** due **October 15**.
- Some students may already qualify via the *ISC Pre-Approved Accommodation List*.

## Financial Assistance (Student Allowance) — Secondary Student Support

Student allowances for eligible students in grades 8-12 (paid directly or used by the First Nation) for books/supplies, specialized clothing (incl. graduation allowance), equipment/specialized educational services, and specialized-class fees. Per-student rates: Ungraded Secondary \$144, Grade 8-10 \$144, Grade 11 \$287, Grade 12 \$431. Adults eligible. *Example:* total 44 students = \$8,486.

- **Report (DCI):** Nominal Roll Report & Education Staff Census Report — due **October 15**.

## Guidance and Counselling — Elementary/Secondary Student Support

Funds K-12 guidance and counselling administration (incl. base salary, benefits, travel, telephone, Nominal Roll). Formula: **\$179 per student/year + \$1,094 per student/year with approved student accommodation services**. Adults (22+) eligible for the base formula but not the accommodation supplement. *Example:* (\$179 × 44) + (\$1,094 × 1) = \$8,970.

- **Report (DCI):** Nominal Roll Report & Education Staff Census Report — due **October 15**.

## Menstrual Products

Funding to purchase and distribute free menstrual products (pads, tampons, cups, dispensers) for all students reported as female or other gender, aged 8+ (incl. adults) on reserve. Rate **\$154 per eligible student** (Nominal Roll). *Example:* 23 students × \$154 = \$3,542.

- **Report (DCI):** Nominal Roll Report & Education Staff Census Report — due **October 15**.

## First Nations and Inuit Cultural Education Centre Program (CECP)

Proposal-driven funding for First Nation and Inuit Cultural Education Centres to develop/promote First Nations, Inuit, and Innu culture — reviving traditional skills, developing contemporary skills, supporting traditional languages, and promoting cross-cultural awareness. Allocations are consistent with historical funding; new recipients funded by BC Region subject to availability. Centres previously funded via the First Nation Confederacy of Cultural Education Centres (FNCCEC) apply to FNCCEC (www.fnccec.ca); others apply to the BC Regional office (due May 7).

- **Apply (PAW # 515410):** Cultural Education Centres (CECP) Program Proposal — due **May 6**.
- **Report (DCI # 515786):** CECP Program Report — due **June 30** (FNCCEC-funded centres verify requirements with FNCCEC).

## Post-Secondary — PSSSP and UCEPP

The **Post-Secondary Student Support Program (PSSSP)** provides non-repayable support for First Nations (registered Indian) students advancing toward a recognized post-secondary credential; the **University and College Entrance Preparation Program (UCEPP)** supports students in accepted entrance-preparation programs. Funding goes directly to First Nations, who administer the programs per National Guidelines. BC General List individuals (and those affiliated with but not members of a BC First Nation) may apply via the BC Association of Aboriginal Friendship Centres (www.bcaafc.com).

- **Funding methodology:** Initial allocation = lesser of reported audited expenditures or a per-capita amount (regional budget ÷ total population). Shortfalls go to a per-First-Nation **Protected Pool** (apply to access); unclaimed residuals go to a **General Pool** for other First Nations. Does not apply to Treaty/Self-Government or block/NFR Grant First Nations (NFR Grant nations excluded from the General Pool from 2024-25).
- **Apply (PAW # N/A):** Post-Secondary Education Protected / General Pool Application — due **May 31, 2024**.
- **Report (DCI # 4016769):** Annual Register of Post-Secondary Education Students — due **August 31, 2024**.
- Support: FNESC PSE Resource Line, 1-877-280-4151 / pse@fnesc.ca.

## Post-Secondary Partnerships Program (PSPP)

First Nations-directed, regionally delivered, proposal-driven program supporting First Nations-established post-secondary institutions and community-based programming, to increase First Nations students pursuing post-secondary education. Administered in BC since 2020-2021 by FNESC with IAHLA (calls for proposals, regional guidelines, PSPP Review Committee). Allocations based on a national formula (First Nations populations 18+) with an AFN-approved base amount per region.

- **Apply (PAW):** Post-Secondary Partnership Program Proposal — *see FNESC for dates.*
- **Report (DCI):** Post-Secondary Partnerships Program Report — *see FNESC for dates.*
- Contacts: ISC Education Programs unit; FNESC pseallocations@fnesc.ca, 604-925-6087 / 1-877-422-3672. Guidelines at www.fnesc.ca/pspp/ and iahla.ca/pspp/.

## Income Assistance First Nations Youth Employment Strategy Pilot (IAFNYES)

Supports on-reserve and Yukon First Nations youth (ages 18-30) receiving ISC Income Assistance to transition to work or return to school through mentored work placements and case-management supports. Co-funded equally by the Income Assistance program and FNIYES; expenses reported to **each** program's funding. Delivered via a targeted call for proposals.

- **Apply (PAW # 1898217):** Income Assistance First Nations Youth Employment Strategy Proposal — due **February 9, 2024**.
- **Report (DCI # 434342 Report / # 434352 Youth Evaluations):** First Nations & Youth Employment Strategy Report — Final Report and Youth Evaluations due **May 15**.
