# Lands & Economic Development

This chapter of the ISC BC Region 2024-2025 Program Guide covers the funding programs administered by the Lands and Economic Development Directorate (LED), which supports sustainable development of First Nations lands, resources, and Indigenous economies while protecting the environment. LED's BC operations span three service areas — Economic Development, Lands Management, and Environment & Natural Resources — and the programs below fund everything from economic development planning and capital projects to reserve land management, land codes, and contaminated-site remediation. Each program entry notes its purpose, eligibility, application path (PAW #), reporting obligations (DCI #), and contacts.

> **Reading the codes:** **PAW #** identifies the application/proposal/work-plan instrument you submit. **DCI #** identifies the data collection (report) you must submit afterward. Both correspond to forms in ISC's reporting systems.

---

## General Information & What's New

Lands and natural resources are among the most valuable assets held by First Nations communities, and their sustainable use is critical to Indigenous economic development. The Lands and Economic Development Directorate (LED) delivers programs in partnership with First Nations, organized into three service areas:

- **Economic Development Unit** — planning and development of economic opportunities plus capital contributions to support jobs and revenues. Programs: LEDSP Core, LEDSP Targeted, CORP.
- **Lands Management** — divided into three units:
  - **Lands Operations** — land management services for First Nations operating under the *Indian Act*: Indian Land Registry (leases, permits, mortgages), individual land holdings (allotments, transfers, cancellations), leasing payment processing, land surveys, and lease/permit compliance monitoring.
  - **Lands Modernization** — capacity building to manage reserve lands and move beyond the *Indian Act*. Programs: RLEMP, FNLM.
  - **Program and Project Support (PPS)** — Additions to Reserve (ATR) and land transactions under Section 35 of the *Indian Act*; ATR funding for economic-development purposes can be authorized via LEDSP.
- **Environment and Natural Resources (ENR)** — environmental protection on reserve and other ISC-jurisdiction lands: contaminated-site management, environmental operations, waste management and capacity-building workshops, environmental assessments under the *Canadian Environmental Assessment Act*, and administration of timber and sand & gravel permits under the *Indian Act*. ENR also facilitates LEDSP-Targeted funding for community projects with potential environmental impact.

ATR policy: <https://www.sac-isc.gc.ca/eng/1332267668918/1611930372477>

## Lands and Economic Development Services Program (LEDSP) — Core

**Purpose:** Provides support to First Nation and Inuit communities that wish to increase their level of participation in their economy.

**What it funds:** Annual allocations supporting operational costs to undertake economic development activities — community economic development planning, capacity development initiatives, proposal development, and financial resource management.

**Who's eligible:** First Nations and Tribal Councils.

**How to apply:** **No application required.** Annual applications are no longer needed; funds are advanced as part of the recipient's annual initial allocation. (First Nations with a Block or NFR Grant funding agreement have this program built into their core funding.)

**What to report:** DCI **471935** — *LEDSP Planned Activities and Report — Section C: Lands and Economic Development — Planned Activities Report.* Due **May 31**.

**Funding methodology:** Base minimum is the higher of **\$15,000** or the weighted per-capita amount based on remoteness.

**Contact:** Economic Development Team — bcledecdev@sac-isc.gc.ca

## Lands and Economic Development Services Program (LEDSP) — Targeted

**Purpose:** Supports projects and initiatives for lands management, environmental management, and economic development activities.

**What it funds (eligible activities):**
- *Lands Support* — initiatives building community control over lands and resources; land designation and processing of administrative instruments (leases, permits) under the *Indian Act*; Additions to Reserve (ATR) projects supporting economic development.
- *Environmental Management Support* — pollution-prevention/waste-management projects on reserve; environmental management "best practices" for land and community assets; improving environmental regulatory compliance on reserve.
- *Economic Development Support* — initiatives to undertake economic development activities, including capacity development and community economic development planning.

**Who's eligible:** Block and 10-year Grant funded applicants are eligible to apply, as LEDSP-Targeted funding is not built into Block funding core budgets.

**How to apply:** PAW **6161886** — *LEDSP Application and supporting documents as may be requested by program staff.* **Intake 1: March 1**; continuous intake after March 1, subject to budget availability.

**What to report:**
- Progress reporting as outlined in the Statement of Work — due as outlined in the SOW.
- DCI **472939** — *Lands and Economic Development Programs Project Status Report.* Due **May 31**.

**Funding methodology:** No funding formula; an application is required.

## Community Opportunity Readiness Program (CORP)

**Purpose:** Provides project-based funding to support communities' pursuit of revenue-generating economic opportunities where the recipient has a community plan in place that supports the project.

**What it funds:**
- Shovel-ready capital projects: community economic infrastructure (e.g., site development supporting multiple businesses), business development (acquisition, start-up, or expansion), and major resource and energy projects.
- Planning stages of capital projects.

**Who's eligible:** Block and 10-year Grant funded applicants are eligible to apply, as CORP funding is not built into core budgets.

**How to apply:** PAW **6161886** (multiple intakes):
- *CORP Prioritization Framework (CORP-PF) Application* for shovel-ready capital projects — **Intake 1: November** (for funding the following fiscal year); additional intakes during the year — contact the Economic Development Unit for due dates.
- *CORP Application* for planning stages of capital projects — **Intake 1: March 1**; continuous intake after March 1, subject to budget availability.
- Funding requests **up to \$250K**: due **March 15**.
- Funding requests **greater than \$250K** (capital projects): due **December 3**.

**What to report:**
- Progress reporting as outlined in the Statement of Work — due as outlined in the SOW.
- DCI **472939** — *Lands and Economic Development Programs Project Status Report.* Due **May 31** (or as identified in the SOW).

**Additional information:** Pre-screening teleconferences with an ISC economic development officer prior to application are highly recommended (arrange via bcecdev@sac-isc.gc.ca). Refer to the CORP summary table in the CORP Guidelines for allowable maximum funding amounts and percentages per project type. Program info: <https://www.sac-isc.gc.ca/eng/1100100033417/1613659339457>

**Funding methodology:** No funding formula; an application is required.

**Contact:** Economic Development Team — bcecdev@isc-sac.gc.ca

## Reserve Land and Environment Management Program (RLEMP)

**Purpose:** Provides funding for First Nations to develop the capacity to manage and exercise increased responsibility over their reserve land, resources, and environment pursuant to the *Indian Act*. Supports operating a lands office while building capacity for land use planning, environmental monitoring, and compliance management; accepted First Nations receive funding to train and certify a professional land manager plus ongoing funding for land/environmental activities.

**What it funds:** Training and certification of a professional land manager; ongoing land and environmental activities; additional funds (subject to availability) for developing a Land Use Plan, Community Environmental Sustainability Plan, and Compliance Framework.

**Who's eligible:** First Nations seeking to manage land/environmental responsibilities under the *Indian Act*. Entry requires submitting an Expression of Interest; if accepted, the First Nation must also submit a Band Council Resolution consenting to all program Terms and Conditions.

**How to apply:** PAW **6978371** — *RLEMP Expression of Interest Form* (for new entrants). Submitted to the nearest ISC BC Regional office; **Ongoing** — no deadline, forms may be submitted at any time.

**What to report:** DCI **10067812** — *RLEMP Activity Report.* Due **May 31**.

**Funding methodology:** Following the Budget 2023 announcement of \$30 million over five years, ISC is implementing a base level of funding for each level of responsibility (starting FY 2023-24). Annual funding is determined by a formula considering population, reserve land area, land instruments registered in the Indian Land Registry System, and community-based plans; different rates apply by level of responsibility (training/development, operational, etc.).

**Contact:** Lands Modernization Team — modernisationdesterres-landsmodernization@sac-isc.gc.ca

## First Nations Land Management (FNLM)

**Purpose:** Enables First Nations to transition away from the 44 land/resource/environmental-management sections of the *Indian Act* via the Framework Agreement on First Nation Land Management, allowing them to develop their own land codes and laws and take advantage of cultural and economic development opportunities. Supported by the Land Advisory Board (LAB) and the First Nation Land Management Resource Centre (FNLMRC).

**What it funds:** Two phases —
- **Developmental phase** — funding to develop a land code, negotiate an individual agreement, and hold a community ratification vote (laid out in a Developmental Phase Funding Agreement, DPFA).
- **Operational phase** — funding to administer and manage lands, resources, and environment under the community's own Land Code (no longer bound by the *Indian Act* land sections).

**Who's eligible / how to enter:** Completion of a **Band Council Resolution** is the first step for consideration; BCRs are accepted on an ongoing basis. PAW: **Not Applicable**.

**What to report:**
- During the **Developmental Phase**, reporting is submitted to the FNLMRC. The DPFA outlines requirements via Annex A (milestones to reach a land-code vote), Annex B (roles and responsibilities), and Annex C (FNLMRC funding toward milestones and reporting requirements — reports go to FNLMRC, available/explained at www.labrc.com).
- There is **no DCI for the Operational Phase**, as FNLM allocations are Grant funding.

**Funding methodology:**
1. Developmental phase funding flows from the Land Advisory Board Resource Centre per the DPFA once the First Nation is formally integrated into FNLM.
2. Operational phase funding is distributed to fully participating FNLM communities at the start of each fiscal year by ISC.

**Contacts:**
- Lands Modernization Team — modernisationdesterres-landsmodernization@sac-isc.gc.ca
- First Nations Land Management Resource Centre — Website: www.labrc.com; P.O. Box 1022, Sutton West, Ontario, L0E 1R0; info@labrc.com; Fax (866) 817-2394

## Federal Contaminated Site Action Plan (FCSAP) & Contaminated Sites On-Reserve (CSOR)

**Purpose:** FCSAP is a federal program (cost-shared between ISC and Environment Canada) addressing contaminated sites on federal lands; the extent of funding and program scope are set year-to-year. Contaminated site management includes ongoing remediation/closure of known sites and identification/assessment of newly discovered sites.

**What it funds:** Three project types — **assessment**, **remediation/risk management**, and **care and maintenance**. BC Region's CSOR program targets sites with high human-health and ecological risks, prioritized via the National Contaminated Site Classification System (Environment Canada). Other environmental issues may be eligible through the LED Directorate. A list of known on-reserve contaminated sites is in the Integrated Environmental Management System (IEMS), updated annually.

**How to apply:** No specific PAW listed (funding prioritized via the national classification system).

**What to report:** DCI **472939** — *Lands and Economic Development Project Status Report.* Due **at 6-month intervals from the date of funding and within 90 days of project completion**.

**Funding methodology:** Not applicable.

**Contact:** Manager, Contaminated Sites — Lands and Economic Development Directorate — 604-404-2045

## Leasing Land on Reserve

**Purpose:** Leasing is one method by which First Nations can create economic activity and employment on reserve. A Band can request that the Minister of ISC authorize leasing of reserve land specifically designated for that purpose; individual Band members allocated reserve land by certificate of possession may also request a lease of their allocated land.

**Reference:** The ISC Lands Management Manual covers the leasing process — see Chapter 5 (land designation) and Chapter 7 (leasing): <https://publications.gc.ca/site/eng/9.840071/publication.html?wbdisable=true>

**Contact:** Lands Operations Team — bclandregistry@sac-isc.gc.ca (registry inquiries) or bclands@sac-isc.gc.ca (general inquiries)

## Land Survey Requests

**Purpose:** ISC has limited funding for land surveys needed by First Nations, with the goal of achieving clear boundary definitions in support of economic development and the settlement of estates.

**What it funds (eligible categories):** Designations for leasing purposes; Additions to Reserve; reserve boundary investigations and/or surveys (e.g., for a Section 50 *Indian Act* sale of estate lands). *Surveys for capital projects are not an approved category — that survey funding can be included in capital project submissions.*

**How to apply:** First Nations submit all known survey needs through an annual **call letter process held each January**; submission requests are due in early January of the following year. Requests are made through a **Band Council Resolution** that also authorizes a surveyor to perform the work, if funded. Subject to available funding.

**Contact:** LED's Survey and Lands Records Officer — bclands@sac-isc.gc.ca

## First Nations Commercial and Industrial Development Act (FNCIDA)

**Purpose:** Provides regulatory support to First Nations pursuing large-scale, on-reserve commercial or industrial projects. It allows a First Nation to request that the federal government develop regulations applying to a specific project on a specific piece of reserve land, duplicating relevant provincial laws/regulations that apply to similar off-reserve projects to fill regulatory gaps.

**Who's eligible:** Projects must (a) identify a regulatory gap, (b) have a commercial or industrial partner, and (c) have formal support from the province.

**How to apply / more info:** <https://sac-isc.gc.ca/eng/1100100033561/1612128155378>
