# Community Development

This chapter of the ISC BC Region 2024-2025 Program Guide covers Governance and Individual Affairs programs that support First Nations governance, capacity enhancement, community-driven planning, and the management of individual affairs. It documents the core funding streams a band administrator works with — Band Support Funding, Employee Benefits, Tribal Council Funding, Professional & Institutional Development, Estates Management, and Registration/Status Cards — along with their application (PAW) and reporting (DCI) requirements, due dates, and key ISC contacts.

## General Information & What's New

Governance and Individual Affairs programs support First Nations governance and capacity enhancement, community-driven planning, and management of individual affairs to facilitate self-determination of communities and individuals.

- **Comprehensive Community Planning (CCP) and the Mentorship Initiative** — CCP is a holistic process engaging community members in planning and implementing the long-term vision for their community. Funding to participate is available through Professional & Institutional Development (P&ID), the First Nation Infrastructure Fund (FNIF), and other non-ISC sources. BC Region holds an annual CCP Workshop delivered by experienced First Nations CCP Champions. The CCP Mentorship Initiative pairs experienced planning champions (mentors) with First Nations in the early stages of planning (mentees); ISC funds both to work together.
  - Contact: **Manager, Community Initiatives** — bc-ccp@sac-isc.gc.ca, 236-330-1562
- **Status Cards** — Individuals registered under the *Indian Act* can apply for a status card. Two versions exist: the paper-laminated Certificate of Indian Status (in-person only via Indian Registration Administrators at band offices) and the Secure Certificate of Indian Status (SCIS) — available via a band-office Registration Administrator, the regional office, or by mail to the SCIS National Processing Unit (see www.canada.ca/indian-status). ISC has trusted-source partnerships (e.g. Lu'ma Native Housing Society, Community Social Planning Council) to help complete SCIS applications: sourcesfiables-trustedsources@sac-isc.gc.ca.

## Band Support Funding (BSF)

**Purpose:** A grant to assist band councils in funding the costs of local governments and the administration of departmentally funded services — including the formation, implementation, and enforcement of policies and regulations, selection of representatives, and efficient delivery of services to citizens in a way that enhances social and economic well-being.

**Eligibility:** All recipients must submit the application, *except* First Nations receiving BSF through a Block or NFR Grant funding agreement (for whom this program is built into core funding) — those need only apply when renewing or entering a new agreement.

**Apply:** PAW **41814** — Band Support Funding Application — due **January 10**.

**Report:** No report due.

**Funding methodology:** Initial allocations are 70% of the previous year's allocation, adjusted after the July Indian Government Support (IGS) database update. Allocations are based on the BSF Band Profile (generated by IGSS); the formula has seven components, each tied to a band-council activity. Funding formula tables: Appendix 4 — https://sac-isc.gc.ca/eng/1100100013828/1565364945375#chp18

**Contacts:**
- Manager, Governance and Capacity Development — 236-330-4999
- Program and Policy Advisor — 604-603-9617

## Employee Benefits (EB)

**Purpose:** (Formerly Band Employee Benefits.) Funds eligible First Nations and Tribal Councils for the employer's share of contributions to eligible employees' pension plans — employer-sponsored pension plans, the Canada/Quebec Pension Plan (CPP/QPP), and additional employee benefits.

**What it funds (limits):** Up to a maximum of 5.5% Private Pension Plan (PPP) of gross salaries, plus current Canada Pension Plan levels, administrative costs, and a maximum of 2% of other benefits.

**Funding factors:**
- **CPP:** reimbursed on the smaller of reported employer contributions, reported employee contributions, [(total salary of eligible employees − (# employees × Basic Exemption)) × current CPP rate], or the Maximum Annual Employee Contribution (\$3,249.79) × # of contributing employees — never below \$0.
- **PPP:** maximum rate **5.50%**; reimbursed on the smaller of reported employer contributions, reported employee contributions, or (total eligible salary × current PPP rate) — never below \$0.
- **Other Benefits** (group health/dental insurance): maximum **2.00%**; total eligible salary × group rate, only if the eligible employee contributes to PPP.
- **Administration cost:** \$125 per employee (under 10 employees), or \$1,000 plus \$25 per employee (over 10 employees).

**Eligibility:** First Nations with a Block or NFR Grant funding agreement have this built into core funding.

**Apply:** PAW **41802** — Employee Benefits Application — due **January 10**.

**Report:** DCI **41784** — Employee Benefits Report (for all recipients except those receiving EB through Block Funding) — due **April 30**.

**Funding methodology:** Initial allocations are 70% of the prior year, adjusted after the July IGS database update; new plans are allocated from the Application for Band Employee Benefits form and the List of Eligible Employees form. To maintain eligibility, the Annual Information Report, Certified Financial Statements, and monthly remittance must be submitted directly to the Office of the Superintendent of Financial Institutions (OSFI); funding may be withheld if OSFI reporting/remittance requirements are unmet.

**Contacts:**
- Manager, Governance and Capacity Development — 236-330-4999
- Program and Policy Officer Advisor — 604-603-9617

## Tribal Council Funding (TCF)

**Purpose:** Supports the core operations of Tribal Councils so they can deliver programs and services to their membership/citizens and support their capacity development.

**Eligibility:** For a work plan to be accepted, the Tribal Council must plan to deliver programs/services and support the capacity enhancement of its member First Nations. Work plans consisting of only core administration costs are not eligible.

**Apply:**
- PAW **5814375** — Tribal Council Funding Application — due **January 10**.
- Current "Certificate of Good Standing" (PAW n/a) — due **January 10**.

**Report:** DCI **5814389** — Tribal Council Funding Report — due **April 30**.

**Funding methodology:** Levels are determined by the value of eligible expenditures in the work plan and by a formula based on three factors: (1) number of member First Nations, (2) total on-reserve population of member First Nations, and (3) number of ongoing major programs delivered. Funding tiers:

| Tier | Total Funding | Eligibility |
|------|---------------|-------------|
| Tier 1 | \$200,000 | Must meet **ALL**: on-reserve population under 2,000; serve two to five First Nations; deliver up to three ongoing ISC programs. |
| Tier 2 | \$350,000 | Must meet **ONE or MORE**: on-reserve population 2,000–5,500; serve six to eight First Nations; deliver three to five ongoing ISC programs. |
| Tier 3 | \$500,000 | Must meet **ONE or MORE**: on-reserve population more than 5,500; serve nine or more First Nations; deliver six or more ongoing ISC programs. |

**Contacts:**
- Manager, Governance and Capacity Development — 236-330-4999
- Program and Policy Officer Advisor — 604-603-9617

## Professional & Institutional Development (P&ID)

**Purpose:** A proposal-based contribution program implemented through projects, developing the capacity of First Nations and Inuit communities to perform core functions of government such as planning and risk management, leadership, and financial management.

**What it funds:** Typical projects include governance training for newly elected leadership, development/updating of financial or HR policies, development/updating of custom election codes, and training in strategic planning. Activities providing training/resources/services for a specific departmental program are *not* eligible.

**Eligibility:** First Nations with a Block and NFR Grant funded agreement are eligible to apply. Applications must include project activities, deliverables, budget, and timeline. Projects must be completed within the fiscal year the funding is received. Applicants may submit more than one proposal per fiscal year and should note each proposal's priority.

**Apply:** PAW **638262** — Project or Work Plan Funding Application (quotes required for Consultant Fees, Purchases and/or Training; Long Term Community Plan optional). Due date: **Call for Proposals due date varies** (call typically open for ~6 weeks); the program continues accepting proposals throughout the fiscal year until the budget is fully allocated.

**Report:** DCI **4548549** — Activity and Expenditure Report (Professional & Institutional Development) — due **May 31**.

**Funding methodology:** An annual call for proposals opens for ~6 weeks. Submissions before the due date are evaluated by the P&ID committee per program terms/conditions and guidelines; community needs, applicant funding history, status of P&ID final reporting, and funding availability are all considered. Proposals received within the call period are considered first; remaining budget allows year-round acceptance. Note: P&ID funding is for activities completed within one fiscal year and should not be relied upon as ongoing revenue.

**Contact:** General Inquiries — bcregionpid@sac-isc.gc.ca. See the P&ID Program guidelines at sac-isc.gc.ca.

## Estates Management, Individual Trust Accounts and Matrimonial Real Property

**Purpose:** The Estates Management Funding Program supports estates-management-related activities.

**What it funds:**
- Developing skills for effective governance of estates programs;
- Activities informing band members on the benefits of estate planning and the importance of writing wills;
- Increasing the role of First Nations in administering the estates of deceased individuals and of those declared (by provincial/territorial laws) incapable of managing their property.

**Apply:** PAW **3869162** — Estates Management Program Proposal — due **TBD**.

**Report:** DCI **4548549** — Activity and Expenditure Report (Estates Management Program) — due **TBD**.

**Funding methodology:** The Regional Estates Unit contacts First Nations directly when funding becomes available. The BC Region Estates Unit recommends proposals for final review and submits to headquarters for final approval.

**Contact:** Manager, Estates — BCEstates@canada.ca, 1-888-917-9977.

## Registration and Status Cards Unit

**Purpose:** Resources enabling bands to provide registration and status card services to community members by employing a Community-Based Registration Administrator (CBRA) directly or through a Tribal Council. CBRAs must be certified by the Department before assuming their duties.

**Apply:** Not applicable (no PAW).

**Report:** Not applicable (no DCI). However, First Nations must report information to the regional office per the *Indian Act* and CBRA training, and must submit supporting documentation plus a data-entry report for every change event.

**Funding methodology:** Funding is only available to retain one or more CBRAs (service may be delivered by the First Nation or Tribal Council). Allocations use the national formula of \$7.07 per capita (registered individuals as of December 31) plus \$15.00 per compensable event processed the previous calendar year; allocations under \$5,000 are topped up to \$5,000.
- *Example:* 800 registered individuals × \$7.07 = \$5,656, plus 20 compensable events × \$15.00 = \$300, total \$5,956.
- *Compensable events* include births, deaths, marriages, divorces, band transfers, and SCIS applications (aided by a CBRA) resulting in a status card. Events reported by one First Nation on behalf of another are split events: the reporting and affected First Nation(s) split the \$15.00.
- First Nations with a Block or NFR Grant funding agreement have this built into core funding.

**Contact:** Registration and Status Cards Unit — bcirbl@sac-isc.gc.ca, 604-775-5100.
