# Funding Information

Chapter 10 of the ISC BC Region 2024-2025 Program Guide is the **financial
reference** chapter. Unlike the program chapters, it does not describe
individual programs with application (PAW) and reporting (DCI) numbers; instead
it documents *how money flows*: the BC Region cash-flow regime, national
cash-flow parameters by funding authority, the GCIMS payment production
calendar, the national funding-agreement models, the full ISC chart of accounts
crosswalk for transfer payments, and a glossary of acronyms. Band
administrators use it to understand when installments arrive, how funding type
(Grant vs. Contribution vs. Block) affects payment timing, and which
cost-centre / BA-FA / activity codes appear on a funding agreement.

> Note on structure: the source PDF does **not** carry per-program purpose,
> eligibility, PAW#/due-date, DCI#/due-date, or named contacts in this chapter —
> those live in the individual program chapters of the guide. The sections below
> faithfully reflect what Chapter 10 actually contains. The general contact for
> cash-flow changes is the recipient's **ISC Program Officer**; reporting
> compliance is enforced automatically by GCIMS under Directive 123.

## What is a targeted program?

- **Purpose:** Defines "targeted" funding — a program subject to annual funding
  availability, delivered only once that year's budget is secured (as opposed to
  ongoing/core funding).
- **Targeted programs listed in the guide:**
  - *Social* — Family Violence Prevention Program (FVPP)
  - *Emergency Management* — Emergency Management Assistance Program, Non-Structural Mitigation & Preparedness (NSMP)
  - *Lands & Economic Development* — Community Opportunity Readiness Program (CORP)
  - *Community Development* — Professional & Institutional Development (P&ID)
  - *Child and Family Services* — Jordan's Principle; Jurisdiction & Engagement (JE); Delegated Aboriginal Child & Family Services Agencies (DACFSA); Community Wellbeing & Jurisdiction Initiative (CWJI)

## Funding Agreement Cash Flow Regime

- **Purpose:** Sets out how the BC Region distributes the *initial allocation* in
  funding agreements with First Nations and Tribal Councils, so recipients meet
  cash-flow needs while respecting the Treasury Board Transfer Payments
  Directive (s. 6.4) cash-management guidelines.
- **Key mechanics:**
  - Funding may be **pooled** and released on receipt/approval of required
    applications, reports, operational and work plans (e.g. Tribal Council
    Application & Work Plan, capital projects).
  - Recipients may adjust expenditure plans to actual cash flow — contact the
    relevant **Program Officer** to discuss changes.
  - GCIMS may **automatically halt funds** for any Prior-Year overdue reports
    (Directive 123, Reporting Management).

### Community Infrastructure – Capital Operations & Maintenance
- Most O&M activities pay **8.333% per month** (8.337% in March), 100% of
  formula: Wastewater (Q35A), O&M Operator Retention (Q35D), Water (Q35B), Fire
  Protection (Q3BG), Roads & Bridges (Q3BH), Electrical (Q3BI), Community
  Buildings (Q3BK), Other Protection (Q3BM), Maintenance Management (Q3B0),
  Solid Waste (Q3BT), Training–Fire Protection (Q3BN), Teacherages (Q371).
- **Municipal Services (Q3BJ):** 16% Apr, then 12%/month.
- **Block Funding:** 25.3% Apr, then 8.3%/month — all activities in the
  recipient's core funding.

## National Cash Flow Parameters Defined for Funding Authorities

- **Purpose:** National rules tying installment timing to **funding type**
  (Grant vs. Contribution) and **dollar value**.
- **Grants:** over \$1M = monthly over 12 months; \$500,001–\$1M = quarterly
  (Apr/Jul/Oct/Jan); \$100,001–\$500,000 = semi-annual (Apr/Oct); up to \$100,000
  = one installment (Apr).
- **Contributions (Set/Fixed/Flex):** over \$500,000 = monthly over 12 months;
  \$250,001–\$500,000 = 25% Apr then monthly Jul–Mar; \$100,001–\$250,000 =
  quarterly; \$25,000–\$100,000 = 75% Apr + quarterly (Jul/Oct/Jan); up to
  \$24,999 = 90% Apr / 10% Sep.
- **Block Contribution Funding (CFNFA Management Policy):** 25.3% Apr, 8.3% for
  nine months May–Jan.

## BC Region GCIMS 2024-2025 Production Cycle

- **Purpose:** The month-by-month payment-run calendar (Regular, Supplementary,
  PAYE Release) with every internal deadline a payment must clear.
- **Tracked deadlines per run:** Programs enter budget allocations into the NTCF
  tool (4:00 PM) → Budget Allocation approved in GCIMS (last day of transfers) →
  TPO sends amendments to recipients → recipient amendment deadline (NOON) →
  transfer-payment deadline to Accounting Hub (NOON) → Section 34 to
  Inter-Sector / Cost Centres / authorization → Section 34 approval to Accounting
  Hub → Section 33 completed and sent to SAP.
- **Use:** Band administrators back-plan submissions; e.g. an April regular run
  required NTCF entry by 26-Jan and recipient amendments by 15-Mar, with SAP
  completion 22-Mar.

## National Funding Agreement Models 2024-2025

- **Purpose:** Per the TBS Directive on Transfer Payments, a funding agreement is
  the written agreement between Canada and a recipient setting out obligations
  for transfer payments and maintaining the ISC–recipient accountability
  relationship.
- **Published models:**
  - Provincial/Territorial Governments Funding Agreement Model 2024-2025
  - Comprehensive Funding Agreement (with 10-year grant) 2024-2025
  - Funding Agreement Model: Other 2024-2025

## ISC Chart of Accounts Reference for Transfer Payments

- **Purpose:** The complete 2024-2025 ISC chart-of-accounts crosswalk used on
  funding agreements — maps **Cost Centre → Fund → BA/FA → Description → Funding
  Type → Coding Crosswalk** for every transfer-payment activity.
- **Coverage:** Block coding and the Contribution/NFR-Grant crosswalks, including
  Financial Transfer Agreement (378), New Fiscal Relationship grant streams
  (476–479), CIRNA-coded consultation/self-government/claims activities, FNIH
  health contributions (mental wellness, healthy living, healthy child
  development, public health), Lands & Economic Development (LEDSP, FNLM,
  contaminated sites), Community Infrastructure capital & O&M (water/wastewater,
  housing, education facilities, Gas Tax / Building Canada funds), Registration &
  Membership / Treaty Annuities, Governance & Band Employee Benefits, and Child &
  Family Services / Jordan's Principle.
- **Use:** Reconcile funding-agreement line items, confirm activity (Q-code) →
  funding type (Block / Fixed / Flex / Set / Grant), and trace the
  contribution-coding crosswalk.
