# Community Infrastructure

> Chapter 3 of the ISC BC Region 2024–2025 Program Guide (pages 27–51).

Community Infrastructure invests in First Nations infrastructure and housing,
provides funding to mitigate health and safety risks, and promotes responsible
asset management. It groups programs under three streams — **Community
Facilities**, **Water and Wastewater Systems**, and **Operations & Maintenance
(O&M)** — with capital projects flowing from a community's First Nations
Infrastructure Investment Plan (FNIIP). This reference summarizes each program:
its purpose, what it funds, eligibility, how to apply (PAW # + due date), what
to report (DCI # + due date), and key contacts.

## General Information & What's New

**Project submission in-boxes (BC Region):**

- Capital projects: `Projetsdimmobilisationscb-capitalprojectsbc@sac-isc.gc.ca`
- Housing: `HousingBC-logementcb@sac-isc.gc.ca`

Reference materials (application checklists, terms of reference, the *Practical
Guide to Housing Support*) are posted on the Naut'sa mawt Tribal Council website.

**Key 2024–2025 updates:**

- **School Space Accommodation Standards (SSAS)** — updated standards in effect
  since April 1, 2023, applying to new schools and major renovations entering a
  feasibility study. Existing allocation procedures and funding levels are
  unchanged. Questions go to your Capital Management Officer (CMO).
- **First Nations Housing Management & Capacity Enhancement funding** —
  confirmed through end of 2026–2027 with a **5% annual escalator** on the
  2023–24 amount. Flexible use for housing-related positions/activities.
- **Tendering Policy (interim update)** — Interim Update to the Tendering Policy
  on Federally Funded Capital Projects has been in effect since August 1, 2021;
  a more comprehensive review is underway.
- **Carve-out for Treaty / Self-Government First Nations** — Budget 2022 set
  aside \$565M over five years for Self-Governing and Modern Treaty Holders.
  Recipients of this distinctions-based carve-out are **not eligible** for
  additional targeted ISC infrastructure funding from Budget 2021/2022; they
  should share capital and housing proposals with both ISC and CIRNAC to avoid
  duplication.

## Community Facilities

### First Nations Infrastructure Investment Plan (FNIIP)

- **Purpose:** National ISC planning tool giving a consistent, 5-year approach
  to short- and medium-term capital/budget forecasting based on a First Nation's
  community priorities. It is the gateway for nearly all capital funding — most
  programs require projects to appear in the FNIIP.
- **What it funds (capital categories):** drinking water, housing, education
  facilities, plus the broader CFMP categories. Projects are ranked against
  **national priorities** (mitigating health/safety risks, protecting asset life
  cycles, addressing water/sewage CFMP backlogs, investing in sustainable
  communities and capacity).
- **Eligible:** First Nations; developed with the CMO. Submit at least every
  other year.
- **Apply (PAW #):** **460674** — First Nations Infrastructure Investment Plan
  (FNIIP), due **September 30** (pre-requirement for funding).
- **Report (DCI #):** **41701** — Community Infrastructure and Housing Annual
  Report, due **May 31**; **460674** — FNIIP Annual Report, due **September 30**.
- **Reference:** Protocol for ISC-Funded Infrastructure (PIFI) lists all
  applicable policies, protocols, guidelines, and standards.

### Capital Facilities and Maintenance Program (CFMP)

- **Purpose:** Financial assistance to plan, construct/acquire, operate, and
  maintain community capital facilities and housing, comparable to non-First
  Nations communities of similar size (per the Level of Service Standards / LOSS).
- **What it funds (major capital categories):** water and wastewater, housing,
  education facilities, roads and bridges, structural mitigation,
  electrification, solid waste disposal, fire protection, subdivisions, and
  community buildings. O&M funding is provided annually for existing assets.
- **Eligible:** First Nations and other eligible recipients; projects must be in
  the FNIIP and ranked by asset-category priority.
- **Apply:** Funding applications are required at **each project stage**
  (Feasibility; Pre-Design/Design; Construction/Acquisition), each meeting ISC
  application requirements including LOSS compliance and a technical merit
  assessment. **Life Cycle Cost (LCC)** analysis must demonstrate
  value-for-money across viable options (decentralized systems, MTSAs,
  renovations, etc.).
- **Funding:** Project-based, on an approved cash-flow projection. A **CFMP
  Project Risk Assessment Tool** sets the risk score, determining the
  contribution approach (fixed, flexible, or set) and approval process.
- **Contact:** Capital Management Officer (CMO) — 1-800-665-9320 (toll-free).

### First Nations Infrastructure Fund (FNIF)

- **Purpose:** Complementary funding to CFMP, helping communities develop,
  improve, and increase public infrastructure; also funds **off-reserve,
  cost-shared** projects with non-First Nation partners (municipalities) or other
  Indigenous partners.
- **What it funds:** planning and skills development, solid waste management,
  roads and bridges, energy systems, connectivity, structural mitigation, and
  cultural/recreational facilities.
- **Eligible / how to apply:** **No application** — projects are identified,
  reviewed, and selected by ISC regional offices from the FNIIP and priority
  ranking. Priority may go to high-needs communities, regional-impact projects,
  cost-shared/own-source-supplemented projects, and those aligned with regional
  priorities or partnerships.
- **Report (DCI #):** **460671** — Capital Projects Report: Progress Report (at
  6-month intervals from date of funding); Completion Report (within 90 days of
  project completion, may include certificate of completion, legal survey,
  as-built drawings, ICMS Asset Inventory Forms / former CAIS forms).
- **Contact:** CMO — 1-800-665-9320 (toll-free).

### Structural Mitigation Program

- **Purpose:** Disaster-mitigation projects to save lives and reduce damage to
  community infrastructure (e.g., floodways and dykes against floods).
- **What it funds:** projects that reduce the severity of / control a natural
  hazard, or measures reducing existing infrastructure's susceptibility to
  natural hazards.
- **Apply (PAW #):** No separate PAW — projects must be **included in your
  FNIIP** to be considered. Prioritized via the Structural Mitigation Program
  Funding Framework and ranked nationally using the **Structural Mitigation
  Ranking Tool (SMRT)**.
- **Report (DCI #):** **460671** — Capital Projects Report: semi-annual progress
  reports (6-month intervals from funding); completion reporting within 90 days
  of completion.
- **Contact:** CMO — 1-800-665-9320 (toll-free).

### Education Assets and Facilities

- **Purpose:** Education capital for design, construction, and upgrading of
  band-operated elementary/secondary schools on reserve; ranking centers on
  immediate health and safety needs.
- **What it funds:** new schools and renovations, assessed via the **School
  Priority Ranking Framework (SPRF)** using variables like nearest-school
  location and Nominal Roll populations. New projects must consider the SSAS.
- **Eligible:** First Nations with school projects identified in their FNIIP. If
  approved, a **formal application is required**. Factors may include cost
  sharing, alternative procurement (design-build), and modular/prefab construction.
- **Apply (PAW #):** No separate PAW — projects must be **included in your FNIIP**.
- **Report (DCI #):** **460671** — Capital Projects Report: semi-annual progress
  reports (6-month intervals; required for projects not completed within 180 days
  of funding); completion reporting within 90 days of completion.
- **Contact:** Regional Program Advisor – Education Infrastructure —
  (236) 330-7399.

### Housing — Housing Support Program (HSP)

- **Purpose:** Funding support (via subsidies) along a continuum of sustainable
  housing — from planning to construction — to help develop and implement
  longer-term housing strategies.
- **What it funds (two applications):**
  - **Governance & Capacity Application** — creation of housing policies/plans,
    training, and capacity enhancement.
  - **Housing Support Project Application** — flat-rate subsidy toward new home
    construction/purchase, regular and health & safety renovations, energy
    efficiency, inclusive of building inspection costs.
- **Eligible:** First Nations only — **not individual members** (members should
  work through their First Nation administration). Projects must be in the FNIIP
  in priority order; funding is subject to available budgets. Block / NFR Grant
  agreement holders allocate a portion of capital core to housing.
- **Apply (PAW #):** No separate PAW — projects must be **included in your FNIIP**.
- **Report (DCI #):** **460671** — Capital Projects Report for Housing Projects,
  due **June 30 of the following fiscal year**; accompanied by a Housing
  Inspection Report (authorized by a qualified inspector certifying code
  compliance) and a Summary of Financial Expenses.
- **Notes:** All construction must meet/exceed current BC building and fire
  codes; inspectors should be BOABC-certified (www.boabc.org). Other agencies
  (CMHC, BC Housing, First Nations Market Housing Fund) may also support.
- **Contact:** Regional Housing Officer — 1-800-665-9320 (toll-free).

### Ministerial Loan Guarantee (MLG)

- **Purpose:** ISC provides loan security so lenders will finance on-reserve
  housing loans to First Nations or individual Band members for construction,
  acquisition, or renovation.
- **Apply (PAW #):** Applications for MLGs must be submitted to ISC by the First
  Nation, for both **individual and Band** housing projects (no listed due date).
- **Contact:** Regional Housing Officer — 1-800-665-9320 (toll-free).

### Extended Asset Condition Reporting System (E-ACRS)

- **Purpose:** All on-reserve assets registered in the Integrated Capital
  Management System (ICMS, formerly CAIS) and receiving O&M funding are
  **inspected every three years** (April–September) by qualified contractors to
  identify maintenance needs. In 2024–2025, inspections cover **50 First Nations
  in northeast and central BC**.
- **What it funds:** ISC provides additional funding for **E-ACRS Group 2
  Priority Projects** — health/safety repairs or replacements of water/wastewater
  components, fire protection assets, and school facilities — with eligibility
  varying by funding-arrangement type (Regular, Block, Grant, Block Grant; see
  the eligibility matrix on page 40).
- **Apply (PAW #):** E-ACRS Group 2 Priority Projects Funding Application Form,
  due **May 30**. Eligibility by arrangement type per the matrix.
- **Report (DCI #):** **4548549** — Activities and Expenditure Report: E-ACRS
  Group 2 Priority Projects, due **May 31**.
- **Funding:** Allocated based on E-ACRS cost estimates or current contractor
  quotes.
- **Contact:** Maintenance Systems Officer — 604-655-4720.

## Water and Wastewater Systems

### First Nations Water and Wastewater Enhanced Plan (FNWWEP)

- **Purpose:** Five-year plan delivering program enhancements — assessment of
  existing facilities, increased training via the Circuit Rider Training Program,
  policy modifications for small water/septic systems, and water/wastewater
  service agreements.
- **What it funds / eligibility:** Capital for community water and wastewater
  systems; financial support available for systems serving community housing on
  Band lands of **at least five homes**.
- **Apply (PAW #):** Approved FNIIP plus a project submission, subject to the
  capital approval process (no separate due date).
- **Report (DCI #):** **460671** — Capital Projects Report: progress report
  (6-month intervals; minimum for projects not completed within 180 days of
  funding); final/completion report within 90 days of completion.
- **Funding:** Project-based per the FNIIP; priority to high-risk projects in the
  regional FNIIP.
- **Contact:** Manager, Specialist Services Unit — 604-360-6512.

### Water and Wastewater Systems (supporting programs)

- **Safe Drinking Water & Wastewater Systems:** A priority for BC Region. Canada
  has committed at least **\$6 billion (2021–2030)**, at ≥ \$400 million/year, to
  fund construction, upgrading, operation, and maintenance of on-reserve water
  infrastructure and end long-term drinking water advisories.
- **Annual Performance Inspections:** Required under ISC water/wastewater
  protocols to track each system's risk ranking in ICMS; in BC, conducted by
  consultants.
- **Circuit Rider Training Program (CRTP):** Long-term capacity program providing
  on-site training/mentoring to drinking-water and wastewater operators to
  achieve and maintain certification.
- **Operator Training and Certification Program:** Complements CRTP with
  classroom training leading to certification under the **Environmental Operators
  Certification Program (EOCP)**.

## Operations & Maintenance (O&M)

### General Information — O&M Additional Funding Requests

- **Purpose:** Additional O&M funding for **urgent** situations affecting health,
  safety, or security (e.g., a broken main distribution line in a cold snap, a
  failed well pump, a control system knocked out by an electrical surge) —
  repairs beyond the scope of regular maintenance.
- **How to access:** Submit details of required repairs plus cost quotations from
  qualified contractors to your CMO. Block / NFR Grant holders may submit a
  request if they have expended a majority of their minor capital on outstanding
  E-ACRS Group 2 Priority Projects for water, wastewater, and fire protection.
- **Contact:** CMO — 1-800-665-9320 (toll-free).

### Operation and Maintenance (O&M) of Infrastructure Assets and Facilities

- **Purpose:** Ongoing support to operate and maintain community facilities and
  assets (community buildings, water treatment systems, fire trucks, etc.).
- **Eligibility / how to access:** The capital asset must be **recorded in ICMS**
  — the First Nation submits Completion Documents (record drawings + completed
  ICMS Asset Inventory Form) to add the asset, after which ISC funds O&M going
  forward. O&M is captured annually in non-Block/non-Grant agreements; Block /
  NFR Grant holders have it built into core funding.
- **Funding (cost-shared levels — ISC / First Nation):**

  | Asset type | ISC | First Nation |
  |---|---|---|
  | Teacherages | 100% | 0% |
  | Fire protection | 90% | 10% |
  | Roads/bridges | 90% | 10% |
  | Community electrical services | 90% | 10% |
  | Water/wastewater | 100% | 0% |
  | Solid waste | 80% | 20% |
  | Dykes | 80% | 20% |
  | Community buildings | 45% | 55% |

  O&M is determined by formula: **Total Calculated O&M Cost = Base Unit Cost ×
  City Centre Index × Zone Index × Asset Quantity**, then × % ISC Contribution =
  Total Contribution Funding. School O&M is funded under the BC Tripartite
  Education Agreement (BCTEA).
- **Contact:** Maintenance Systems Officer — 604-655-4720.

### Municipal Type Service Agreements (MTSA)

- **Purpose:** Supports purchasing essential municipal services from a
  neighboring local government, private contractor, individual, or third party.
- **What it funds:** water supply/distribution, wastewater collection/disposal,
  fire (structural) suppression, garbage collection/landfill fees, recycling,
  street lighting, 9-1-1 dispatch, and railway-crossing maintenance (on roads to
  residential areas). Only the portion serving **Band member residences** is
  funded, at 100% of eligible costs.
- **Apply (PAW #):** MTSA Application Template, due **July 26, 2024**.
- **Report (DCI #):** **No reporting requirements.**
- **Funding:** 100% of MTSA funding (non-block/non-grant) recalculated each
  April 1 from the prior year's allocation plus a **2% annual adjustment**.
  Communities may apply for more if eligible costs rose ≥ 10%. Block / NFR Grant
  holders have MTSA built into their annual agreement, if applicable.
- **Contact:** Allocation Officer / Project Coordinator — 1-800-665-9390
  (toll-free).

### Fire Protection

- **Purpose:** CFMP funding support for fire protection services; mitigates
  health/safety risks and protects facility/housing lifecycles.
- **What it funds:** An annual subsidy of **\$60 per household** (Tier 1 home /
  Tier 2 community fire protection) for prevention training, home fire-safety
  inspections, smoke/CO alarms and extinguishers, volunteer training, and other
  eligible activities. Capital items (fire halls, trucks, equipment, tools) must
  come through the FNIIP. ISC may also subsidize **90%** of calculated O&M costs
  for fire halls, fire boxes, and fire trucks.
- **Programs:** **Fire Safety Assessment (FSA)** program assesses community fire
  protection on a **three-year cycle**. ISC partners with the **First Nations'
  Emergency Services Society (FNESS)** (www.fness.bc.ca/fire-services) for
  training, alarm/extinguisher distribution, public education, governance
  workshops, and firefighter certification — delivered at no cost.
- **Apply:** Capital requests from an FSA must be identified in the community's
  FNIIP. The \$60/household subsidy is formula-allocated from the Housing and
  Infrastructure (H&I) database (prior-year house counts).
- **Eligibility note:** Block / NFR Grant recipients must use core funding for
  fire protection activities.
- **Contact:** Regional Program Advisor – Fire Protection — (604) 314-1491.
