# General Information

The opening chapter of the Indigenous Services Canada (ISC) – British Columbia Region **2024–2025 Program Guide**. It is a user-friendly quick-reference orienting First Nations administrators, program managers, and ISC staff to how the Guide works and to the mechanics shared across every program: contacting the region, the types of transfer payments and funding agreements, how to submit Proposals/Applications/Work plans (PAW) and reports (DCIs), and the systems used to manage them. This chapter contains no program-specific funding lines — those appear in later chapters — but it defines the PAW and DCI concepts and submission channels referenced throughout the Guide.

> In the event of a variance, your **ISC funding agreement, the Reporting Guide, and ISC's Program Manuals take precedence over this Guide.** All information is current as of publication; mid-year changes are communicated directly to Chief and Council / Indigenous Organizations and reflected in the online version on the AFOABC website (<https://afoabc.org/publications-2/>). The Guide is reviewed and updated every two years by ISC BC Region staff.

## Overview of Transfer Payments

Defines how ISC moves money to recipients. A **transfer payment** is a transfer of goods, services, assets, or money to a third party where the Government acquires nothing directly in return. Governed by the Treasury Board **Policy on Transfer Payments (PTP)** and **Directive on Transfer Payments**.

ISC uses two types:

- **Grants** — subject to eligibility in the program's terms and conditions. Recipients are accountable to their members; an audit by the Department is not required (though reporting on activities/results may be). *Since April 1, 2019, a 10-year grant covering core funding is available to eligible First Nations — contact your Funding Services Officer (FSO).*
- **Contributions** — subject to performance conditions in a funding agreement. Recipients are accountable to Canada and are **subject to audit**.

**Reference links:** PTP <https://www.tbs-sct.gc.ca/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=13525> · Directive <https://www.tbs-sct.gc.ca/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=14208> · ISC Terms & Conditions <https://www.sac-isc.gc.ca/eng/1386868587379/1618143863185>

## Overview of Funding Agreements

A **funding agreement** is the binding legal contract transferring federal funding to eligible recipients to deliver programs, services, and activities. ISC maintains generic models used for different purposes.

National models for 2024–2025 (links in the Funding Information section of the Guide):

- First Nations and Tribal Councils National Funding Agreement Model 2024–2025
- Streamlined Funding Agreement Model for First Nations (new/optional) 2024–2025
- Funding Agreement Model – Other 2024–2025

**Comprehensive Funding Agreement (CFA):** Introduced April 1, 2019, it adopts the language of the **New Fiscal Relationship (NFR)** and includes **all funding approaches: Set, Fixed, Flexible, Block, Grant, and NFR Grant.**

**Reference:** <https://www.sac-isc.gc.ca/eng/1545169431029/1545169495474>

## Proposal, Application & Work plan (PAW) and Data Collection Instrument (DCI) Information

Defines the two core document types used across all programs.

- **PAW (Proposal, Application, Work plan):** Standardized forms ISC uses to collect, from funding recipients, the results they intend to achieve with the funding requested. Used to support statutory requirements, resource allocation, performance reporting, accountability, program planning, policy analysis, and operations.
  - **Apply via (templates):** <https://www.sac-isc.gc.ca/eng/1571829044381/1571829074923>
- **DCI (Data Collection Instrument):** The form of a reporting requirement. The specific DCIs a recipient must submit — with their **due dates** — are listed in the **"Reporting and Due Dates" Schedule/Annex of the recipient's funding agreement** (including any Amending Agreements).
  - **Report templates available from:** the Reporting Guide (<https://www.sac-isc.gc.ca/eng/1573764124180/1573764143080>), GCIMS, or the Indigenous Services Portal.
  - Reports may be submitted in **PDF or online**.
- **Notification of Reporting Requirements Report:** A monthly email from the Department giving report statuses (overdue, due soon, revisions required, pending review); also viewable directly in GCIMS by recipients with access.
- **Key contact / caution:** A **Funding Services Officer (FSO)** or **Program Manager** answers specific questions on reporting requirements and applications. **Failure to meet reporting requirements set out in the agreement's terms and conditions may result in automated halted funds** — contact your FSO.

### PAW and DCI Submission Options

Send **one copy** of each report via one of three channels:

1. **Upload directly to GCIMS** — if you have access. Max file size 100 MB. Upload a PAW under *Funding Request → Documents* (green plus sign); upload a DCI by searching for the report and clicking the green plus sign.
2. **Email to `bcreports@sac-isc.gc.ca`** — use a **separate email per report/application** with the report attached, and CC your FSO and the Program Contact. Required **subject-line format:** `Recipient # , Report name or acronym, DCI # (if applicable), fiscal year`.
   - *Example:* `0123, Annual Register of Post-Secondary Education Students, DCI # 4016769, 2022-2023`
   - You will receive an email confirmation; if none arrives, contact your FSO.
3. **Upload through the EIS Portal (Education only)** — if you have access, you can upload, electronically review, and submit Education-related PAWs and DCIs (e.g., the Nominal Roll Student and Education Staff Census Report).

### Grants and Contribution Information Management System (GCIMS)

A web-enabled transfer-payment management system that automates the Department's transfer-payment processes, manages funding-agreement information, and gives First Nations and other recipients online access. Benefits:

- Tracking of submitted reports with immediate feedback on issues that might trigger funding halts.
- Electronic access to reports for statistical analysis of program information.
- Electronic access to cash-flow and payment information.
- Electronic access to funding-agreement terms and conditions.
- Ability to check the status of amendments or budget adjustments.

**Request access** through your FSO. Reference: <https://www.sac-isc.gc.ca/eng/1100100010038/1618138097980>

## Snapshot — ISC Contribution Funding Approaches

Comparison of the six funding approaches across recipient eligibility, agreement duration, in-year reallocation, and carry-forward of unexpended funds.

| Element | SET Contribution | FIXED Contribution | FLEX Contribution | BLOCK Contribution | Grant | NFR Grant |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Recipient type** | Any eligible recipient per program T&Cs | Indigenous recipients only | Indigenous recipients only | Indigenous recipients only | Any eligible recipient per program T&Cs | First Nations meeting eligibility requirements |
| **Eligible programs** | All contribution transfer-payment programs | As specified in program guidance | As specified in program guidance | As specified in program guidance | Activities authorized for Grant funding | Programs authorized for NFR Grant |
| **Agreement duration** | No duration stipulations | No duration stipulations | Min. 2 fiscal years, typically up to 5 | Typically 5 years | Typically up to 5 years | As requested by recipient, up to 10 years |
| **Reallocation during fiscal year\*** | With written agreement from Canada, within same Budget Activity | Provided activities delivered in the fiscal year, reallocate within same Budget Activity | Reallocate within the same Program Inventory | Provided mandatory activities delivered, reallocate within Block Activities; may reallocate to Set/Fixed/Flexible/Grant with written agreement from Canada | No reallocation across grant programs | May reallocate to meet priorities and desired outcomes |
| **Carry forward of unexpended funds** | No retention; unexpended funding recovered | Recovered, except where delivery requirements are met and funds are used next FY for consistent purposes (or per accepted plan) | Recovered, except funds remaining at end of each FY may be used next FY toward program results | Mandatory activities delivered: if other Block activities, no plan; if outside the Block, per plan submitted within 120 days of year-end and accepted by Canada | Retain unexpended funds | Retain and use per Recipient's Strategic Plan; may be recovered if no longer eligible |
| **Duration of carry forward** | No carry forward | Use in next subsequent FY (year after agreement expiry) | Use in any subsequent FY, only within the agreement term | Use in any subsequent FY, including after agreement expiry | — | — |

\* May **not** reallocate any funding from **Jordan's Principle – Child First Initiative.**
