# Social & Partnership Programs

> Chapter 6 of the ISC BC Region 2024-2025 Program Guide (pages 102-128).

ISC BC Region's Social Support Programs help First Nations and organizations
administer benefits and services to individuals and families living on reserve.
The suite has three pillars — **Income Assistance**, **Assisted Living**, and the
**Family Violence Prevention Program** — plus several partnership/capacity
funding streams (Disabilities Initiative, UPIP, BOC). This chapter is a
reference for band administrators: each section below captures purpose, what is
funded, eligibility, how to apply (PAW # + due date), what to report (DCI # +
due date), and key contacts.

**Default support contact for Income Assistance / Assisted Living programs:**
BC Region Band Social Development Worker (BSDW) Policy Support Line —
tsdbsoutien-bsdwsupport-bc@sac-isc.gc.ca · 1-888-440-4080.

**Common reporting note:** Block-funded recipients report on a risk-based
frequency derived from their General Assessment (GA) scores; First Nations with
a Block or NFR Grant funding agreement have most Income Assistance / Assisted
Living programs built into their core funding.

# Income Assistance

The Income Assistance (IA) Program is a last-resort component of Canada's social
safety net, providing funds to individuals and families ordinarily resident on
reserve to cover the basic expenses of daily living (food, clothing, shelter)
once all other means of income have been exhausted. It aligns with provincial
income assistance programs off-reserve.

**Cross-cutting items:**
- **National Directive on Shelter Allowance:** Clarifies administration of
  Shelter Allowance to on-reserve IA clients and the Universal/Community Wide
  Rental Regime. Info via the National Shelter Directive / BSDW line.
- **Income Assistance Family Supports (IAFS):** Former National Child Benefit
  Reinvestment (NCBR) activities, now in the IA budget for IA clients and
  dependents on reserve. Funds: Child Nutrition, Support to Parents, Cultural
  Enrichment, Home-to-Work Transition, Childcare. Funded at the same level/
  activities as NCBR in 2016-2017; reallocation between activities via the
  Funding Services Officer.

## Basic Needs

- **Purpose / funds:** Last-resort funding for eligible individuals and families
  to cover basic living expenses. Covers Basic Support Allowance; Persons with
  Persistent Multiple Barriers (PPMB) designation/assistance; Persons with
  Disabilities (PWD) designation/assistance; and Shelter Allowances (actual
  documented rent, utilities, and limited home maintenance). See Handbook
  Vol. 1, Chs. 4, 5, 8.
- **Eligibility:** Individuals/families ordinarily resident on reserve who have
  exhausted other income sources.
- **Reporting:** DCI **455897A** – Income Assistance Report, quarterly Jul 30 /
  Oct 30 / Jan 30 / Apr 30 (Block-funded = risk-based on GA scores). DCI
  **33315098** – NFR Grant Income Assistance Block Funding Recipients,
  annually Jul 29.
- **Funding methodology:** Allocations based on previous year's audited
  expenditures, adjusted by IA Reports/forecasts; paid as reimbursement of
  eligible expenditures. Built into core funding for Block/NFR recipients.
- **Contact:** BSDW Policy Support Line.

## Children Out of the Parental Home Assistance (Grand parented)

- **Purpose / funds:** Financial support for a child living on-reserve in the
  home of a relative when the parents have placed the child in that relative's
  care. **Closed to new applicants since March 31, 2012** — only grand-parented
  clients (assessed under pre-2012 policy) are administered by BSDWs.
- **Eligibility:** Grand-parented COPH clients only. Each adult (18+) in the home
  must complete the enhanced screening consent form; BSDW submits it to MCFD's
  Centralized Screening Hub (fax 250-387-7756 / CSH.GeneralEnquiries@gov.bc.ca).
  See Handbook Vol. 1, Ch. 6.
- **Provincial service options:** Delegated Aboriginal Children and Family
  Service Agency; MCFD Extended Family Program; MCFD Client Relations Branch
  (1-877-387-7027).
- **Reporting:** DCI **455897A** – Income Assistance Report, quarterly
  Jul 30 / Oct 30 / Jan 30 / Apr 30.
- **Funding methodology:** MCFD performs screening checks; BC Region reimburses
  MCFD directly from the Basic Needs budget. Allocations from prior-year audited
  expenditures; reimbursement of actual expenditures. Built into core funding for
  Block/NFR recipients.
- **Contact:** BSDW Policy Support Line.

## Non-Status Health Benefits

- **Purpose / funds:** Health benefits for eligible non-status, on-reserve IA
  recipients with Medical Service Plan and Fair Pharma Care coverage. Includes
  Dental, Orthodontia, Optical Services, Health Related Travel, Medical
  Equipment. See Handbook Vol. 1, Ch. 10.
- **Eligibility:** Non-status, on-reserve IA recipients. Benefits are limited and
  rate-scheduled. **Expenditures over \$500 require Funding Services Officer
  approval via form "SA-205 Non-Status Health Benefit Request & Authorization".**
- **Reporting:** DCI **455897A** – Income Assistance Report, quarterly
  Jul 30 / Oct 30 / Jan 30 / Apr 30.
- **Funding methodology:** Funded under Basic Needs (see IA – Basic Needs). Built
  into core funding for Block/NFR recipients.
- **Contact:** BSDW Policy Support Line.

## Hardship Assistance

- **Purpose / funds:** A distinct category of income assistance with its own
  eligibility criteria, meeting basic needs only up to maximum rates (Handbook
  Vol. 1, Ch. 7).
- **Eligibility:** Assessed only after a client has been tested for and found
  ineligible for regular basic IA; applicants must use all other available
  resources first.
- **Reporting:** DCI **455897A** – Income Assistance Report, quarterly
  Jul 30 / Oct 30 / Jan 30 / Apr 30.
- **Funding methodology:** Funded under Basic Needs (see IA – Basic Needs). Built
  into core funding for Block/NFR recipients.
- **Contact:** BSDW Policy Support Line.

## Other Benefits

- **Purpose / funds:** Other benefits for a client's physical/social well-being:
  Child Benefits; Family Bonus Top-up; Supplementary Assistance to Old Age
  Security; Comforts Allowance; Winter Supplement; BC Bus Pass; Funeral Costs;
  Transportation Support; Natal Supplement; Diet Supplement; Short-Term, Monthly
  and Tube Feed Nutritional Supplements; Clothing Supplement for People in
  Special Care Facilities. See Handbook Vol. 1, Ch. 11.
- **Reporting:** DCI **455897A** – Income Assistance Report, quarterly
  Jul 30 / Oct 30 / Jan 30 / Apr 30.
- **Funding methodology:** Funded under Basic Needs (see IA – Basic Needs). Built
  into core funding for Block/NFR recipients.
- **Contact:** BSDW Policy Support Line.

## Work Opportunity Program (WOP)

- **Purpose / funds:** Enhances independence and employability of IA recipients
  by combining their IA funds with other sources to create a project of up to 12
  months providing employment experience to an otherwise-unemployed recipient.
  See Handbook Vol. 1, Ch. 12.
- **Reporting:** DCI **455897A** – SET and BLOCK Contribution Funding Recipients
  Income Assistance Report, quarterly Jul 30 / Oct 30 / Jan 30 / Apr 30.
- **Funding methodology:** Funded under Basic Needs (see IA – Basic Needs). Built
  into core funding for Block/NFR recipients.
- **Contact:** BSDW Policy Support Line.

## Service Delivery (Income Assistance administration)

- **Purpose / funds:** Funds to assist in administering the Income Assistance
  program.
- **Reporting:** DCI **455897A** – Income Assistance Report, quarterly
  Jul 30 / Oct 30 / Jan 30 / Apr 30.
- **Funding methodology:** Formula based on common factors:
  1. **Base Allocation** — Band Support Funding (BSF) on-reserve population in the
     Service Unit (SU) FUNCTION × \$60,184 (annual base of one service unit).
  2. **Caseload Allocation** — BASIC NEED caseloads per SU (one SU = one full-time
     BSDW) plus Child Out of Parental Home (COPH) caseloads per SU.
  Built into core funding for Block/NFR recipients.
- **Contact:** BSDW Policy Support Line.

## Special Needs

- **Purpose / funds:** Special allowances for goods/services for a client's
  physical/social well-being or to support pre-employment: Community Volunteer
  Supplement; Crisis Supplement (formerly Special Needs Allowance); Essential
  Utilities Crisis Supplement & Utility Security Deposits; Emergency/Disaster
  Supplement; Pet Damage Deposits; Guide Animal Supplement; Camp Fee Supplement;
  School Start-up Allowance; Confirmed Job Supplement; Moving/Transportation/
  Living Costs; Identification Supplement. See Handbook Vol. 1, Ch. 9.
- **Reporting:** DCI **455897A** – SET and BLOCK Contribution Funding Recipients
  Income Assistance Report, quarterly Jul 30 / Oct 30 / Jan 30 / Apr 30.
- **Funding methodology:** Geographic "weighted" per-capita formula — First
  Nation's Total Registered Population (Indian Registry codes 1-6 as of Dec 31
  prior year) × Remoteness Index Factor. (2019-20 weighted per-capita = \$18.15.)
  Built into core funding for Block/NFR recipients.
- **Contact:** BSDW Policy Support Line.

## Pre-Employment Supports Program (PES)

- **Purpose / funds:** Case management and pre-employment supports for 49 First
  Nations delivered through 8 sites (CCATEC, Nuu-chah-nulth Tribal Council,
  Okanagan Indian Band, PGNAETA, Seabird Island Indian Band, Sts'ailes, TRICORP,
  Cowichan).
- **Eligibility:** Clients aged 18-64 (no one-year-employable expectation since
  2018), "ordinarily resident on reserve" and "expected to work" in the 49
  communities; includes PPMB- or PWD-designated individuals. A **Band Resolution
  from First Nation Leadership is required** to participate; subject to available
  funding.
- **Reporting:** DCI **455897A** – Income Assistance Report PES (Aggregate Based
  Reporting), quarterly Apr 30 / Jul 30 / Oct 30 / Jan 30.
- **Funding methodology:** Eight sites allocate based on predetermined IA
  populations in their catchment. All First Nations/orgs with funding agreements
  receive allocations as targeted funding.
- **Contact:** BSDW Policy Support Line.

## Income Assistance First Nations Youth Employment Strategy Pilot (IAFNYES)

- **Purpose / funds:** Supports on-reserve and Yukon First Nations youth aged
  18-30 receiving IA to transition to work or return to school via mentored work
  placements plus case-management supports. Co-funded equally by ISC's IA program
  and the First Nations and Inuit Youth Employment Strategy (FNIYES); actual
  expenses are reported to each program's funding.
- **How to apply:** PAW **1898217** – Income Assistance First Nations Youth
  Employment Strategy Proposal, due **February 9** (targeted call for proposals).
- **Reporting:** DCI **434342** (Report) and DCI **434352** (Youth Evaluations) –
  First Nations & Youth Employment Strategy Report; Final Report and Youth
  Evaluations due **May 15**.
- **Contact:** BSDW Policy Support Line.

# Assisted Living

## Adult In-Home Care Services & Service Delivery

- **Purpose / funds:** Two programs — (a) administration of the Assisted Living
  program, and (b) non-medical "Adult In-Home Care"/homemaker services that help
  individuals stay independent at home (housekeeping, meal prep, attendant care,
  adult day care, meals on wheels, psycho-social programs, short-term respite for
  caregivers, non-health transportation). An assessment is required before
  delivering Adult In-Home Care Services. See Assisted Living National Program
  Guidelines 2022-2023 and BC Region Assisted Living Policy Handbook Vol. 2.
- **Reporting:** DCI **455937** – Assisted Living Report, quarterly
  Jul 30 / Oct 30 / Jan 30 / Apr 30 (Block-funded = risk-based on GA). DCI
  **33315098** – New Fiscal Relationship Grant Results Report, annually Jul 29.
- **Funding methodology:** Fixed budget split 85% program / 15% service delivery;
  formula = \$2,000 base per band + per-capita component (registered members aged
  19+ residing on their own reserves). The 15% covers administration of Assisted
  Living services, Adult In-Home Care, Adult Institutional Care, and Adult Family
  Care Homes. In-Home Care deficits are not reimbursed (fixed regional budget).
  Built into core funding for Block/NFR recipients.
  *(Example: 265 adults × \$209.75 + \$2,000 = \$57,584 total → \$48,946 program /
  \$8,638 service delivery.)*
- **Contact:** BSDW Policy Support Line.

## Assisted Living — Adult Institutional Care (AIC)

- **Purpose / funds:** Financial assistance for eligible Indigenous on-reserve
  residents who, by reason of incapacity, require placement in a licensed
  continuing-care facility. Application based; the local Health Authority
  determines nature, amount, cost and duration of service. Provincial Ministry of
  Health Services and ISC BC Region set eligibility.
- **Eligibility / process:** Ideally apply to ISC BC Region before placement; the
  application has four forms — HLTH 1.1, HLTH 1.6, ISC Application Form, Medical
  Release Form — covering health, financial, accredited-facility, age and
  residence assessments. First Nations/Tribal Councils must notify ISC BC Region
  of any change in client status (level of care, facility, finances). ISC BC
  Region approval is required for reimbursement. See National Assisted Living
  Guidelines 2022-2023 and BC Region Assisted Living Policy Handbook Vol. 2.
- **Reporting:** DCI **455937** – SET and BLOCK Contribution Funding Recipients
  Assisted Living Report, quarterly Jul 30 / Oct 30 / Jan 30 / Apr 30. DCI
  **33315098** – New Fiscal Relationship Grant Results Report, annually Jul 29.
- **Funding methodology:** Budgets based on prior-year actual funding requirement,
  adjusted for current-year approved per-client requirements; none set for First
  Nations without reports/approved clients in 2018-2019. Built into core funding
  for Block/NFR recipients. **NFR 10-Year Grant recipients** needing AIC support
  for a new long-term residential care client may (1) complete a new AIC
  application or (2) request an Assisted Living rebase (subject to ISC HQ
  approval) — contact the Assisted Living Program Officer via the BSDW line.
- **Contact:** BSDW Policy Support Line.

## Disabilities Initiative

- **Purpose / funds:** Projects/activities that increase awareness of disability
  issues and available supports and/or improve coordination and accessibility for
  individuals with disabilities on-reserve. Eligible 2024-2025 activities: new/
  innovative client navigator services (including appeals where services denied);
  advocacy, education and awareness campaigns; research and surveys on on-reserve
  disability issues.
- **How to apply:** PAW – **Not Applicable** (no PAW). Funding is proposal-based;
  proposals reviewed by ISC's National Office on recommendation from ISC BC
  Region.
- **Reporting:** DCI **4548549** – Activities and Expenditures Report – Assisted
  Living, due **May 31**.
- **Contact:** BSDW Policy Support Line.

# Family Violence Prevention Program (FVPP)

- **Purpose / funds:** Funds operations of emergency shelters and transitional
  (second-stage) housing to improve safety/security of Indigenous women,
  children, families and 2SLGBTQQIA+ people across Canada (including the North and
  urban centres), plus community-driven family violence prevention proposals.
  Components:
  - **Prevention and Awareness** — culturally appropriate community-driven
    prevention projects (public awareness campaigns, conferences, workshops,
    stress/anger management seminars, support groups) for First Nations, Inuit,
    Métis, urban and 2SLGBTQQIA+ people facing gender-based violence.
  - **Operations and Capacity Building for Shelters and Transitional Housing** —
    daily operations of emergency shelters and second-stage housing serving
    Indigenous women, children and families (First Nations on/off reserve, Inuit,
    Métis, urban, 2SLGBTQQIA+), plus staff training forums, gatherings and
    resource development/distribution.
- **How to apply:** PAW **5664860** – Family Violence Prevention Program (FVPP)
  Project Proposal, **annually January 15**.
- **Reporting:** DCI **455955** – Family Violence Shelter and Transitional Housing
  Annual Report, annually **May 31**. DCI **1063749** – Family Violence Prevention
  Project Annual Report, annually **May 31**.
- **Funding methodology:** Operations funding is formula-based (formula being
  modernized); shelters/transitional homes may apply for additional capacity-
  building and prevention funding. Allocations to First Nations = \$1,000 base per
  band + geographic "weighted" per-capita (Total on/off reserve population, Indian
  Registry codes 1-6 as of Dec 31 prior year × Remoteness Index Factor). BC Region
  has additional violence-prevention/awareness funding (on/off reserve),
  prioritized case-by-case where there is a specific/critical urgent need, links
  to a community plan, or strong community participation.
- **Contacts:** Mercy Mura, Regional Program Development Advisor / Allocation
  Officer — mercy.mura@sac-isc.gc.ca · 604-505-9138 ·
  bcfsppallocationofficer@sac-isc.gc.ca.

# Urban Programming for Indigenous Peoples (UPIP)

- **Purpose / funds:** Supports organizations delivering holistic, culturally
  appropriate programs/services to First Nations (status and non-status), Inuit
  and Métis living in or transitioning to urban centres, and a coordination
  function across 12+ federal departments serving urban Indigenous peoples. A
  portion sunsetted March 31, 2022 but secured for three more years to March 31,
  2025.
- **Funding streams:** Six national grants/contributions streams (Programs and
  Services; Organizational Capacity; Coalitions; Research and Innovation;
  Infrastructure; Housing). **Only the Coalitions stream is managed in BC Region**
  — supporting local multi-jurisdictional collaboration to identify/address local
  needs. BC Region Coalitions partners: Metro Vancouver Aboriginal Executive
  Council (MVAEC), Surrey Urban Indigenous Leadership Committee (SUILC), Prince
  George Coalition, and Victoria Coalition. Other streams: see the ISC UPIP
  website.
- **Reporting:** DCI **10868729** – Urban Programming for Indigenous Peoples
  Report, due **July 29**.
- **Contact:** Mercy Mura, Regional Program Development Advisor —
  Mercy.mura@sac-isc.gc.ca · 604-505-9138.

# Basic Organizational Capacity (BOC) Funding — Representative First Nation Organizations

- **Purpose / funds:** "Core" funding distributed regionally to eligible
  Indigenous Representative Organizations (IROs) that represent their members'
  interests, issues and concerns.
- **Eligibility:** The three eligible BC Region First Nation organizations are the
  Union of BC Indian Chiefs; the First Nations Summit Society; and the BC
  Assembly of First Nations.
- **How to apply:** PAW **1323247** – Basic Organizational Capacity Contribution
  Program Funding Application, due **December 11**.
- **Reporting:** DCI **1323248** – Basic Organizational Capacity Program Annual
  Report, due **April 30**.
- **Funding methodology:** Distributed in equal amounts to the three eligible
  recipients.
- **Contact:** BSDW Policy Support Line.
