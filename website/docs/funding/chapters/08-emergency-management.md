# Emergency Management

ISC BC Region 2024-2025 Program Guide — Chapter 8.

Emergency management is a shared responsibility and a partnership among First
Nations, the federal government, and the provincial government. Indigenous
Services Canada (ISC) supports on-reserve communities through the **Emergency
Management Assistance Program (EMAP)**, which spans all four pillars —
mitigation, preparedness, response, and recovery — to help First Nations build
resiliency, prepare for, respond to, and recover from emergencies such as
wildfires, flooding, and severe weather. When an emergency exceeds a First
Nation's capacity, **Emergency Management and Climate Readiness BC (EMCR,
1-800-663-3456)** is the first point of contact, and ISC reimburses eligible
costs through EMCR.

## General Information & What's New

- **Authority/program:** Emergency Management Assistance Program (EMAP),
  covering Mitigation, Preparedness, Response, and Recovery.
- **First point of contact in an emergency:** EMCR's Emergency Coordination
  Centre, **1-800-663-3456** (day or night). EMCR reimburses eligible
  response/recovery costs; ISC reimburses EMCR and can cover additional
  federally eligible costs not provincially eligible.
- **Building Back Better (BBB) Strategy Guide:** under EMAP, provides additional
  supports during response (e.g., social and cultural supports for evacuees) and
  recovery (e.g., mitigative enhancements post-disaster, support for personal
  losses). Guide:
  <https://www.sac-isc.gc.ca/eng/1534954506773/1535121720820>
- **General inquiries:** ISC BC Region — bcaandc.do@sac-isc.gc.ca

## EMAP — Preparedness & Non-Structural Mitigation

- **Purpose:** Identify potential emergencies and take proactive measures to
  reduce risk (mitigation), and build the capacity to respond efficiently and on
  time (preparedness).
- **What it funds:**
  - *Non-structural mitigation:* risk assessment, hazard mapping, planning, and
    environmental studies. **Structural mitigation (new capital equipment and
    construction) is specifically excluded** and must be funded under other
    authorities (e.g., Capital).
  - *Preparedness:* developing policies, preparing guidelines, conducting
    training, developing/updating emergency plans, and periodic testing through
    simulation exercises.
- **Who's eligible:** First Nations on reserve. ISC's funding complements the
  guidance, training, and workshops EMCR provides at no cost under the Canada–BC
  Emergency Management Services Agreement.
- **How to apply:** **PAW # 6978382** — Emergency Management Non-Structural
  Mitigation and Preparedness Program Application. **Due date:** Based on the
  annual funding call, but applications are accepted on an **ongoing basis**.
- **Funding methodology:** ISC runs an annual funding call and prioritizes local
  efforts through small-scale projects; ongoing applications are prioritized as
  funding becomes available.
- **What to report:** **DCI # 4548549** — Activities and Expenditures Report.
  **Due:** **May 31**.
- **Key contacts:** Emergency Management Duty Officer —
  bcaandc.do@sac-isc.gc.ca, 604-209-9709. First Nations may also contact EMCR
  Regional Offices directly (see Contacts below).

## EMAP — Response

- **Purpose:** Deal with the short-term consequences of an imminent or occurring
  emergency to protect First Nation members from loss of life or injury and to
  protect houses and other infrastructure.
- **What it funds:** Eligible response expenditures approved by EMCR under the
  **Expense Authorization Form (EAF)** process. ISC may reimburse additional
  costs not provincially eligible, including response items in the Building Back
  Better Strategy Guide.
- **Who's eligible:** First Nations on reserve experiencing an emergency; they
  work through EMCR to access response support.
- **How to apply:** No PAW # and **no deadline to apply** — see Funding
  Methodology. In an emergency, contact **EMCR's Emergency Coordination Centre,
  1-800-663-3456** (first point of contact, day or night). EAF forms, claims
  materials, and guidelines are obtained from EMCR Regional Offices.
- **Funding methodology:** EMCR reimburses First Nations for **100% of eligible
  costs** incurred during an on-reserve emergency; ISC then reimburses EMCR.
  Where costs are not provincially eligible, ISC can review them for federal
  eligibility and reimburse First Nations directly.
- **What to report:** No DCI #. Response reimbursements are approved based on
  ISC's determination of eligible expenditures from supporting documentation
  (invoices, time sheets, and proof of payment).
- **Key contacts:** Emergency Management Duty Officer —
  bcaandc.do@sac-isc.gc.ca, 604-209-9709; EMCR Emergency Coordination Centre,
  1-800-663-3456.

## EMAP — Recovery

- **Purpose:** Restore essential community infrastructure to pre-disaster
  conditions as quickly as possible; may include a mitigation component to
  reduce future vulnerabilities.
- **What it funds:** Recovery of on-reserve infrastructure impacted by an
  emergency event. ISC may reimburse additional costs not provincially eligible,
  such as recovery items in the Building Back Better Strategy Guide.
- **Who's eligible:** First Nations recovering from eligible emergency events.
  The provincial platform is the **Disaster Financial Assistance (DFA)** program;
  First Nations may work through EMCR to access recovery supports including DFA,
  but are responsible for completing their own DFA applications by provincially
  set due dates. When an event is **not DFA eligible**, EMCR informs ISC, which
  determines EMAP eligibility and works directly with the First Nation.
- **How to apply:** No PAW # and **no deadline to apply** — see Funding
  Methodology.
- **Funding methodology:** ISC works with communities to ensure recovery efforts
  for on-reserve infrastructure are fully supported after any recoveries from
  insurance payouts, legal actions, and financial assistance from other sources
  (including other federal programs).
- **What to report:** **DCI # 4548549** — Activity and Expenditure Report (with
  supporting documentation such as invoices, time sheets, and proof of payment).
  **Due:** **April 30**.
- **Key contacts:** Emergency Management Duty Officer —
  bcaandc.do@sac-isc.gc.ca, 604-209-9709; EMCR Emergency Coordination Centre,
  1-800-663-3456.
