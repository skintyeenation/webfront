## Executive Summary

Cantatus thanks Skin Tyee Nation for the rigour of its review. The questions
reflect exactly the kind of careful stewardship this engagement is meant to
support, and we have answered each one directly and transparently. Five themes run
through our responses:

- **Fixed-fee protection.** Year 1 (\$72,000) and Year 2 (\$36,000) form a fixed
  two-year scope of \$108,000. The Nation consumes the time it needs, when it needs
  it, without watching chargeable hours. Difficulty of in-scope recovery is a risk
  we priced into the fixed fee, not a source of additional invoices.
- **Current first, then backwards.** Our goal is to bring the current year up to
  date within roughly the first five to six months, giving the Nation a reliable,
  real-time expense and revenue picture, then reconstruct prior years for audit. We
  deliver through a weekly, hybrid-agile cadence so training reaches your team when
  they need it.
- **No hidden costs.** We identify every circumstance that could generate a
  separate invoice, and a realistic five-year projection. Usage-based items are
  billed by Sage; anything out of scope proceeds only with Council's written
  approval first.
- **The Nation owns its data and its system.** The Nation owns its data and its own
  configurations, can export at any time, and its data is hosted in Canada. We are
  amending the engagement letter's intellectual-property language to state this
  plainly.
- **Impact, not just software.** Our measure of success is a visible, measurable
  improvement in the standard of life in the community, and financial governance
  that helps the Board, Chief and Council, and the team manage resources
  effectively. Through strong business-process design, our goal is for the Nation
  to be well on its way to **Financial Management System (FMS) Certification** with
  the First Nations Financial Management Board within five years.

In preparing these responses we identified several places where the engagement
letter should be updated to match our stated intent (e.g. the intellectual-property
and marketplace-product provisions); we are providing those amendments alongside
this response so the signed agreement and our answers are fully aligned.

## A. Year 1 Fees and Historical Recovery

**A1. Provide a detailed estimate of the consulting / recovery / implementation /
training / optimization hours justifying the Year 1 fee of \$72,000.**

Year 1 is a **fixed-fee scope of \$72,000**, deliberately not hourly. This protects
the Nation: the fee is fixed regardless of how many hours the work takes, so the
Nation can consume as much of our team's time as it needs without watching a meter.
Our commitment is to the outcome, not a number of hours.

*Why the commitment spans two years.* This is not a standard implementation.
Reaching the Nation's goals — getting current, recovering multiple prior years for
audit, and transferring knowledge and training so the team can run the system
independently — realistically takes this long to do properly. The two-year fee
covers the full scope: historical data recovery, implementation to Go-Live, ongoing
optimization, advisory services, and support.

*How the work is delivered.* Through our **DIME Framework** (Discovery,
Configuration, Testing, Training, Go-Live, Optimization), run as a weekly cadence in
a hybrid-agile model — each week we meet with the Nation's team, leave defined
homework, and take away the items our team owns.

**A2. If historical records are significantly worse than anticipated, what
additional costs / delays / out-of-scope activities could arise?**

If the records are worse than expected, recovery takes longer, **but the Cantatus
fixed fee does not increase** — difficulty of in-scope recovery is a risk priced
into the fixed fee. What can change is timeline and a small number of external /
usage-based costs outside our control.

*Does NOT generate an additional Cantatus invoice:* additional recovery,
reconstruction, configuration, training, reporting, or dashboard effort caused by
poor data quality (in scope, fixed); helping the Nation locate/organize/make sense
of difficult data.

*Can change (flagged before incurred):* **timeline** (managed with the auditor and
ISC); **AP Automation usage credits** (bulk historical processing can exceed the
500 included monthly credits; overage \$0.65/credit, billed by Sage — see Section
D); **source documents the Nation cannot produce** (auditor may require alternative
procedures — an auditor cost, not Cantatus); **work the Nation chooses to add
outside scope** (proceeds only with Council's written approval first, at \$300/hr
standard / \$250/hr nonprofit).

**A3. Identify what work is performed by Cantatus, Nation staff, auditors, and
third-party contractors.**

*Cantatus performs:* system configuration for all **9 entities** (base + 8, per
Appendix D), chart of accounts, dimensions/fund structure, AP/AR/GL setup, AI &
automation rules, matching/creation rules, financial reports, dashboards, and
ISC-aligned consolidated reporting design; data extraction & migration from **Adagio
and Sage** where accessible, bank-statement import and reconciliation setup, and
technical reconstruction of multi-year records; all training for finance staff and
named users, ongoing advisory and support, and process/SOP design.

*Nation staff complete:* the physical sorting and scanning of the paper trailer
(Cantatus advises but does not perform the sorting); providing source documents
(bank statements, invoices, receipts, trust records), answering questions, and
reviewing/approving/flagging errors on AI-generated entries.

*Auditors complete:* the independent audits for the outstanding years (≈2020
forward), audit opinions, and any alternative procedures. Cantatus produces
audit-ready files and works directly with the auditor; we do not perform the audit.

*Third-party contractors may be required for:* marketplace products the Nation signs
for separately (**Orchid EFT, AccessPay**; see Appendix C / Section C). The
part-time reporting consultant already assisting the Nation is a Nation-side
resource, not a Cantatus cost.

## C. AccessPay and Banking Costs

**C1. Difference between the AccessPay bank-connection references in Appendix C vs
Appendix D.**

- **Appendix D — native Sage Intacct bank feeds (included).** The subscription
  includes up to **200 bank-account connections** through native feeds (electronic
  access to thousands of institutions for reconciliation), included in the
  \$37,569.60 subscription.
- **Appendix C — AccessPay (separate paid marketplace integration).** A third-party
  bank-feed product priced separately (\$2,500/yr base for 1 connection + 25
  accounts).

*Why both:* native feeds are the default and used first, but as Canadian banks have
tightened security (MFA, token management, session controls) native feeds have
become unreliable for some institutions; AccessPay is a more robust, secure
purpose-built fallback. It is in Appendix C because, based on the Nation's banking
environment, Cantatus expects it will be required — but billing begins only after
validating what native tools reliably handle. If a product proves unnecessary, the
Nation does not pay for it.

**C2. How many billable AccessPay connections are estimated?**

Determined by how many of the Nation's banks native feeds support reliably
(confirmed early). The Nation's environment spans **RBC, BMO, First Nations Bank,
and trust accounts**. Plan for: **Likely 1–2 billable connections** (base + 1–2
additional, each from \$1,300/yr); **Maximum 3** if every primary institution
requires it. Base covers up to 25 accounts; additional accounts from \$20/yr each
only beyond 25.

**C3. Annual AccessPay budget over five years.**

- **Low (base only):** ~\$2,500/yr, +~5%/yr.
- **High (base + two additional connections):** ~\$5,100/yr, +~5%/yr.

Matches the AccessPay range in the B4 projection. The Nation only incurs AccessPay
cost for connections actually deployed because a native feed could not do the job.

## D. AP Automation

**D1. How many invoices do 500 AP Automation credits represent?**

Credits are consumed per action (AP Bill = 1, header-level matching = 1,
non-matching = 1, line-level matching = 2). A simple bill ≈ 1 credit; a typical
matched bill ≈ 2; a line-matched PO invoice ≈ 3+. So **500 credits/month ≈ 165–500
invoices, realistically 200–300/month** for a typical mix — comfortable for normal
go-forward operations at the Nation's size.

**D2. Anticipated AP Automation overages during historical recovery, and magnitude.**

Historical catch-up is a **one-time** event. Two funding options: (1) **per-credit
overage** on the 500-credit tier (\$0.65/credit) — variable, no long-term
commitment, best for a temporary spike; (2) **committing to a higher tier**
(1,000/2,000 credits/mo) — lower per-credit economics but a **five-year subscription
commitment that cannot be downgraded**, appropriate only if ongoing volume will
permanently exceed 500/month.

*Recommendation:* fund catch-up through **per-credit overage**, not a five-year
higher-tier lock-in for a temporary need. **Estimated one-time magnitude ≈
\$3,000–\$6,000**, concentrated in the heaviest catch-up months of Year 1, confirmed
against actual volumes. Backlog is sequenced to avoid unnecessary spikes.

**D3. Annual AP Automation overages once the backlog is cleared.**

Normal monthly volume should fall within the included 500 credits, so **routine
overages are not expected and no tier commitment is needed**. Only if ongoing volume
consistently exceeds 500/month would a higher tier be recommended, as a standalone,
transparent, multi-year decision.

## F. Ownership, Data and Intellectual Property

**F1. What is owned by Cantatus vs Skin Tyee Nation.**

*The Nation owns:* **all of its data** (every financial record, transaction, vendor,
document, report output) and **its own configurations** (chart of accounts,
dimensions, fund structure, dashboards, reports, workflows) — built for the Nation,
belong to the Nation, free to use and change.

*Cantatus retains:* its **reusable IP** — methodologies, frameworks (e.g. the DIME
framework), proprietary templates, configuration libraries, accelerators, tools, and
general know-how developed across many clients.

The distinction: what is specific to the Nation is the Nation's; what is reused
across the practice remains Cantatus's.

**F2. Can the Nation freely modify workflows / dashboards / reports / templates /
smart rules without Cantatus approval?**

**Yes** — day to day, without seeking approval. It is the Nation's system and data;
self-sufficiency is the point. The engagement letter's IP clause exists only to
protect Cantatus's *reusable* assets from being extracted, redistributed, or resold
to third parties — not to control the Nation's use of its own system. Cantatus
recognizes the clause currently reads more broadly than intended and **is amending
the engagement letter** to state plainly: the Nation freely uses and modifies its own
configurations and data; the only restriction is not redistributing/reselling
Cantatus's reusable proprietary methodology and tools to outside parties.

**F3. On exit, what data can be exported, in what format, at what cost?**

- **Ownership:** the Nation owns its data outright; leaving changes nothing.
- **Export anytime** (not only at exit) via Sage Intacct's native export/reporting
  tools, in standard formats (CSV/Excel and standard report outputs) — self-service,
  **no charge while subscribed**.
- **Format:** GL, AP, AR, transactions, vendors, reports all export in standard,
  portable formats.
- **Data residency:** hosted **in Canada**; Cantatus can arrange written confirmation
  of Canadian data residency from Sage if Council wants it documented (subject to Sage
  confirmation).
- **At exit:** because data is owned and exportable any time, leaving is clean; bulk
  or structured migration can be scoped as a service, but basic export the Nation can
  always do itself, for free.

## G. Contract Risk and Exit Rights

**G1. Would Cantatus consider more balanced early-termination provisions?**

Yes — genuinely open, and would welcome a conversation with Council. Context on the
structure: the **\$108,000 over two years** reflects fair value for the full scope
(implementation, multi-year historical recovery, training, advisory). Rather than a
large up-front lump sum, the fee is spread into level monthly payments to ease cash
flow — a benefit to the Nation, not a lock-in. The risk Cantatus protects against is
completing the bulk of work early (say within 15 months) and then having the
engagement treated as "finished, so payment stops"; the fee is for the agreed
two-year value regardless of delivery speed.

*One important distinction on "term."* Two separate commitments: the **Cantatus
professional-services commitment is two years (Years 1–2)**; the **Sage Intacct
subscription is a separate five-year (60-month) term**, and that term secures the
Nation's pricing protection. The Nation is free to choose a shorter Sage term. The
trade-off (a real cost difference, not a sales tactic):

- The current 60-month term locks in the **33.8% blended discount** and the **5%
  annual increase cap** for five years.
- That 33.8% discount is tied to the five-year commitment; without it, from **Year 2
  onward** the Nation reverts to Sage's **standard 20% not-for-profit discount**,
  giving up ~13–14 points (subject to Sage confirmation).
- The **5% cap no longer applies** — renewal increases on a shorter term are uncapped
  and could be ~10% (subject to Sage confirmation).

So declining the five-year term is not felt in Year 1; it lands in Year 2+ as both a
lower discount and uncapped increases (see the Supporting Exhibit).

**G2. Termination-for-convenience / graduated fees / wind-down / review periods used
with other clients?**

The clearest protection is already on the Nation's side: a **material-breach
provision** lets the Nation terminate if Cantatus fails to deliver. Cantatus is open
to structured exit mechanisms, wind-down arrangements, staged approaches, or review
points where they fit a client's circumstances, and would prefer to shape these with
Council live rather than commit to a fixed formula in writing here.

**G3. Flexibility if Council / funding / circumstances change.**

"A great deal." Cantatus understands Nations operate within funding cycles and
changing priorities, and commits to come to the table in good faith — adjusting pace,
rescheduling, or restructuring — if circumstances change materially.

## H. Partnership, Capacity Building and Long-Term Value

**H1. What differentiates Cantatus from other Sage Intacct partners?**

- **Finance specialists who understand Indigenous Nations** — multiple entities,
  diverse funding streams, ISC reporting, audit expectations, and the Schedules of
  Remuneration and Expenses for Chief and Council, designed in from day one.
- **CPA-led recovery and audit-readiness experts**, not just configurators — able to
  reconstruct multiple years of damaged/incomplete records and produce audit-ready,
  ISC-compliant consolidated statements.
- **A local, high-touch BC partner** — hands-on, weekly cadence, direct access to
  senior people.

The value is the combination of all three in one team.

**H2. Measurable value beyond software implementation.**

The deeper purpose is a visible, measurable impact on the standard of life in the
community — strong financial systems are the means, not the goal. Concretely:
financial-governance models that empower the Board, Chief and Council, and finance
team with real-time visibility; a clear line from financial control to community
investment; accountability that strengthens trust with membership and funders
(transparent ISC-compliant reporting incl. the Schedules of Remuneration and
Expenses); and audit readiness/restored control as the foundation.

**H3. Skills transfer, mentorship, capacity building, training, self-sufficiency.**

Self-sufficiency is a primary goal: **business-process design** (controls, SOPs,
transaction flows, approvals, reconciliations); **embedded weekly knowledge transfer**
(train while building, in the flow of real work); **role-based training + the Sage
Intacct Learning Membership**; **documented processes and SOPs**; **AP Automation and
go-forward training**. The explicit aim: by the end of the engagement the Nation can
run its financial system independently — and the strong processes/controls/reporting
are the foundation supporting progress toward **FMS Certification with the FMB** under
the *First Nations Fiscal Management Act*.

**H4. Five years out, what would Cantatus hope Skin Tyee Nation would say?**

That Cantatus helped the Nation take full ownership of its financial story; that
clean, trusted numbers gave leadership confidence to govern resources well and invest
in the prosperity of their people; that the Nation is well on its way to FMS
Certification with the FMB; that the relationship strengthened the Nation's financial
sovereignty and capacity and made a visible, measurable difference to the standard of
life in the community — remembered "not as a vendor who installed a system, but as a
partner who helped the Nation build that capacity for itself."
